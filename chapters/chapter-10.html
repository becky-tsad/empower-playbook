Understanding Unit Economics

Most poverty programs hide behind aggregate numbers. "We served 500 families" sounds impressive but tells funders nothing about efficiency or impact. We don't do that. The Empower model operates with full transparency on unit economics because funders deserve to know exactly what their money buys.

The 7.1:1 ROI Calculation

This isn't an estimate. It's calculated from actual outcomes across 500+ families over three years. Here's how the math works:

Direct Economic Returns (Per Family)

Average annual wage increase +$8,500
Benefits retained (optimized, not lost) +$4,200
Reduced emergency service use +$2,100
Employer retention value (reduced turnover) +$3,400
Local economic multiplier effect (1.3x) +$2,060
Total Annual Economic Value Created $20,260

$20,260 value created / $2,847 cost = 7.1:1 ROI

Funding Sources & Strategy

The Empower model runs on braided funding--multiple sources woven together for sustainability. No single funder carries more than 35% of total program cost. That's by design.

Recommended Funding Mix

Employer Investment

25-30% of total

Certified employers invest $500-2,000/month based on company size and employee utilization. Their ROI: reduced turnover, higher productivity, and a stable workforce.

Foundation Grants

30-35% of total

Multi-year capacity grants from regional and national foundations focused on economic mobility, workforce development, and rural innovation.

Government Contracts

20-25% of total

TANF flexibility funds, WIOA contracts, Community Services Block Grants, and state workforce development allocations. TANF flexibility funds can directly fund navigator positions and benefits cliff tools.

Fee-for-Service

15-20% of total

Poverty IQ certification fees, consulting for other regions, and technical assistance contracts.

Funder-Specific Value Propositions

For National Foundations

The Empower model delivers what most rural programs cannot: a replicable, evidence-based approach with proven ROI. Urban-centric models fail in low-density communities. This is rural-native infrastructure built to scale.

  • Scale opportunity: 85% of U.S. counties are rural. That's 46 million Americans living in poverty or ALICE status
  • Systems change: This isn't a direct service grant. It's an investment in replicable infrastructure that transforms how entire regions move families out of poverty
  • Learning agenda: Built-in data collection and evaluation drive continuous improvement and model refinement

For State Government

Tennessee and similar states face a workforce crisis. Employers can't find workers. Working families can't escape the benefits cliff. The Empower model solves both problems at once.

State Policy Innovations Supporting Economic Mobility

DC Career MAP

5-year transitional assistance model letting families keep benefits while building income

Maine STEP Bill

75% earned income disregard--families keep more of what they earn while transitioning off benefits

Colorado

100% earned income disregard--the gold standard for benefits cliff mitigation

Medicaid Expansion States

Dramatic coverage increases for treatment access, removing a major cliff barrier

  • Workforce pipeline: 72% of families achieve wage increases, expanding the pool of workers earning livable wages
  • Benefits efficiency: Optimized benefit transitions mean state dollars fund advancement, not dependency
  • Rural equity: A proven model that works where traditional workforce programs fail

For Employers

Every HR director knows this: turnover is expensive. Replacing one hourly employee costs $4,700 on average. Families in the Empower model stay 23% longer than non-participants at the same companies.

  • Direct ROI: Every $1,000 invested in Empower saves employers $2,100 in reduced turnover and absenteeism
  • Competitive advantage: The "Empower Certified" designation attracts workers looking for employers who invest in their people
  • Community investment: Employer participation strengthens the workforce pipeline for every local business

For Community Foundations

Local foundations struggle to fund systemic change. Most grants fund programs, not infrastructure. Empower is different: it funds the connective tissue that makes every other program in your region more effective.

  • Leverage existing investments: Navigator coordination makes every other funder's dollars work harder
  • Visible, local impact: Real-time dashboards show community-level outcomes as they happen
  • Sustainability path: Employer and government funding reduce long-term dependence on philanthropy

Sustainability Timeline

The goal: philanthropic independence within 5 years. Here's how funding evolves:

Path to Sustainability

Year 1-2 Foundation-Heavy Launch

60% foundation grants, 25% government, 15% employer. Prove the model. Collect outcome data.

Year 3-4 Diversification

35% foundation, 30% government contracts, 25% employer investment, 10% fee-for-service. Outcome data unlocks government contracts.

Year 5+ Earned Revenue Majority

20% foundation, 30% government, 30% employer, 20% fee-for-service. Foundation grants shift to innovation and expansion--not keeping the lights on.

Making the Ask

Lead with outcomes, not activities. Here's the structure that converts:

  1. The problem (with local data): X families in your region are one car repair away from crisis
  2. Why current approaches fail: Benefits cliffs punish progress. Services are siloed. Programs were designed for cities
  3. The Empower difference: A navigator model with a proven 7.1:1 ROI in a similar rural community
  4. The specific ask: $X investment to serve Y families with Z expected outcomes
  5. The sustainability path: Show how this investment builds infrastructure, not dependence
"We're not asking you to fund a program. We're asking you to invest in infrastructure that makes every other dollar in this region work harder for working families."
