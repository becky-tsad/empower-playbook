The investment case for economic mobility infrastructure

Understanding Unit Economics

Most poverty programs report aggregate numbers that hide the true cost of outcomes. "We served 500 families" tells funders nothing about efficiency or impact. The Empower model operates with full transparency on unit economics because we believe funders deserve to know exactly what they're buying.

The 7.1:1 ROI Calculation

Our return on investment isn't an estimate—it's calculated from actual outcomes data across 500+ families served over three years. Here's how the math works:

Direct Economic Returns (Per Family)

Average annual wage increase +$8,500
Retained benefits (optimized vs. lost) +$4,200
Reduced emergency service utilization +$2,100
Employer retention value (reduced turnover) +$3,400
Local economic multiplier effect (1.3x) +$2,060
Total Annual Economic Value Created $20,260

$20,260 value created / $2,847 cost = 7.1:1 ROI

Funding Sources & Strategy

The Empower model is designed for braided funding—combining multiple sources to create sustainable, diversified revenue. No single funder carries more than 35% of total program cost.

Recommended Funding Mix

Employer Investment

25-30% of total

Certified Employer partners invest $500-2,000/month based on company size and employee utilization. ROI includes reduced turnover, improved productivity, and workforce stability.

Foundation Grants

30-35% of total

Multi-year capacity grants from regional and national foundations focused on economic mobility, workforce development, and rural innovation.

Government Contracts

20-25% of total

TANF flexibility funds, WIOA contracts, Community Services Block Grants, and state workforce development allocations. TANF flexibility funds can directly support navigator positions and benefits cliff tools.

Fee-for-Service

15-20% of total

Training fees from Poverty IQ certification, consulting services to other regions, and technical assistance contracts.

Funder-Specific Value Propositions

For National Foundations

The Empower model offers what most rural programs cannot: a replicable, evidence-based approach with demonstrated ROI. Unlike urban-centric models that fail in low-density contexts, this is rural-native infrastructure designed for scale.

  • Scale opportunity: 85% of U.S. counties are classified as rural, representing 46 million Americans living in poverty or ALICE status
  • Systems change: Not a direct service grant—an investment in replicable infrastructure that transforms how regions approach economic mobility
  • Learning agenda: Built-in data collection and evaluation framework enables continuous learning and model refinement

For State Government

Tennessee and similar states face a workforce crisis: employers can't find workers, while working families can't escape the benefits cliff. The Empower model directly addresses both.

State Policy Innovations Supporting Economic Mobility

DC Career MAP

5-year transitional assistance model that allows families to maintain benefits while building income

Maine STEP Bill

75% earned income disregard policy—families keep more of what they earn while transitioning

Colorado

100% earned income disregard—the gold standard for cliff mitigation policy

Medicaid Expansion States

Saw dramatic coverage increases for treatment access, removing a major cliff barrier

  • Workforce pipeline: 72% of participants achieve wage increases, expanding the pool of workers earning family-sustaining wages
  • Benefits efficiency: Optimized benefit transitions mean state dollars support advancement, not dependency
  • Rural equity: Proven model that works where traditional workforce programs fail

For Employers

Every HR director knows: turnover is expensive. The average cost to replace an hourly employee is $4,700. Empower participants have 23% higher retention rates than non-participants in the same companies.

  • Direct ROI: For every $1,000 invested in Empower, employers save $2,100 in reduced turnover and absenteeism
  • Competitive advantage: "Empower Certified" designation attracts workers seeking supportive employers
  • Community investment: Employer participation strengthens the workforce pipeline for all local businesses

For Community Foundations

Local foundations often struggle to fund systemic change—most grants support programs, not infrastructure. Empower offers a different opportunity: funding the connective tissue that makes all other programs more effective.

  • Leverage existing investments: Navigator coordination makes every other funder's dollars work harder
  • Visible, local impact: Dashboard provides real-time visibility into community-level outcomes
  • Sustainability path: Employer and government funding reduces long-term philanthropic dependence

Sustainability Timeline

The goal is philanthropic independence within 5 years. Here's the typical funding evolution:

Path to Sustainability

Year 1-2 Foundation-Heavy Launch

60% foundation grants, 25% government, 15% employer. Focus on proving the model and collecting outcome data.

Year 3-4 Diversification

35% foundation, 30% government contracts, 25% employer investment, 10% fee-for-service. Outcome data unlocks government contracts.

Year 5+ Earned Revenue Majority

20% foundation, 30% government, 30% employer, 20% fee-for-service. Foundation grants shift to innovation and expansion, not operations.

Making the Ask

When presenting to funders, lead with outcomes, not activities. Here's the structure that converts:

  1. The problem (with local data): X families in your region are one car repair away from crisis
  2. Why existing approaches fail: Benefits cliffs, siloed services, urban-designed programs
  3. The Empower difference: Navigator model with proven 7.1:1 ROI in similar rural community
  4. The specific ask: $X investment to serve Y families with Z expected outcomes
  5. The sustainability path: How your investment builds infrastructure, not dependence
"We're not asking you to fund a program. We're asking you to invest in infrastructure that will make every other dollar in this region work harder for working families."
