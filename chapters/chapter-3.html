The Proof

Promises don't move families out of poverty. Results do. Here's what 923 families across 14 rural Tennessee counties prove when you stop managing poverty and start solving it.

3.1 Empower Upper Cumberland Outcomes

Not projections. Not pilot numbers from a small sample. Verified outcomes from a 4-year, $25 million state-funded initiative across 14 rural Tennessee counties. Real families. Real money. Real proof.

Who These Families Are: Stranded Talent, Not Crisis Cases

69% of Empower families were already working when they enrolled. These are ALICE families—Asset Limited, Income Constrained, Employed—earning too much for assistance but too little to cover basic needs. They weren't in crisis. They were stuck. Hard work should pay off. The Empower Method makes sure it does—unlocking stranded talent by clearing the path from working poverty to economic stability.

3.2 Third-Party Validation

We didn't grade our own homework. Two independent research organizations evaluated Empower. Here's what they found.

MEF Associates ran a rigorous RCT—Empower families vs. a control group receiving standard services. The results speak for themselves.

An 11% difference sounds modest—until you do the math. That's 11% more families permanently exiting poverty compared to the best available alternative. At scale, that's thousands of families who would have stayed trapped. The system punishes effort. This method rewards it.

ROI Analysis

The ROI calculation uses standard social program evaluation methodology: reduced government benefit costs, increased tax revenue, and projected lifetime earnings gains.

Every $1 invested in Empower returns $7.10—through reduced benefit costs, increased tax revenue, and lifetime earnings gains for families.

This isn't charity math. This is economic infrastructure math. Every dollar invested returns seven. The methodology aligns with approaches used by the Urban Institute and comparable research organizations.

The model works across populations and settings.

Atlanta Fed CLIFF Coaching Research

When families see their benefits cliff mapped out clearly, they act. The Atlanta Fed's research confirms what we see every day: benefits cliff navigation isn't just helpful. It's transformational.

Critical Research Finding: Duration Matters

Navigator relationships of less than 6 months produce NEGATIVE outcomes. Short-term interventions don't just fail—they actively harm families. They start processes families can't finish. They create dependency without building capacity. This is why the Empower Method requires 18-24 month commitments. Anything less is worse than nothing.

3.3 Adaptability Proof Points

The Empower Method didn't just work in one place for one population. It's already proving out in new contexts with new agencies.

HRA and DD Agency Adoption

Two major Upper Cumberland agencies—the Human Resource Agency (UCHRA) and the Development District (UCDD)—are adopting core Empower elements for their workforce development families. Early indicators show:

  • The navigator-led approach works for non-TANF populations too
  • Benefits cliff navigation matters across income levels
  • The data dashboard breaks down coordination silos between agencies

Recovery Program Integration

Empower and recovery programs do the same fundamental work—they just meet people at different stages. Recovery focuses on stabilization: sobriety, housing, mental health. But what happens after someone stabilizes? Without a clear path to livable-wage careers, relapse rates climb and families cycle back into crisis.

The Empower Method fills that gap. The navigator doesn't show up just for job placement. They walk alongside the person from the beginning—building trust during stabilization, then guiding them step by step into employment, benefits cliff navigation, and long-term economic mobility. One consistent relationship. That's the difference. The navigator coordinates across recovery programs, workforce agencies, and community supports so nothing falls through the cracks. When a crisis hits, the navigator is already there—connecting the person to the right support before a setback becomes a relapse.

The result: a seamless bridge from recovery to economic independence. One trusted person. One coordinated journey. No more handing families off between disconnected programs.
