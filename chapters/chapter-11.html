Adapting the Model

The Four Non-Negotiables work anywhere families face poverty. Here's how to deploy them in any agency, workplace, or school system.

The Empower Method didn't start in rural Tennessee and stop there. The Four Non-Negotiables are systems infrastructure. They define HOW you coordinate support, not WHAT support you provide. That makes them portable. Any organization working with families navigating economic instability can use them.

11.1 Social Services Agency Adaptations

Community Action

Human Resource Agencies (HRA)

Download HRA Guide

Community Action Agencies and HRAs already serve ALICE families across multiple programs. They have the pieces. The Empower Method gives them the coordination layer that connects everything.

How the Four Non-Negotiables Apply:

1

Benefits Cliff Navigation

Use existing LIHEAP, weatherization, and housing data to map the full benefits picture. Model the cliff before making any employment referral.

2

Navigator Model

Convert case managers into navigators. The key shift: one person stays with the family across ALL programs. No handoffs.

3

Wraparound Coordination

HRAs already touch housing, energy, and food. Empower points every service toward one goal: a livable-wage career.

4

Data Dashboard

Unify program databases. Build a single family view. Track outcomes, not enrollments.

Key Insight: HRAs have the programs. They lack the connective tissue. Empower turns a pile of services into a system.

Recovery Services

Substance Abuse Recovery Programs

Download Recovery Guide

Recovery stabilizes. Empower builds the economic path forward. They're the same work at different stages. Recovery gets families standing. Empower gets them moving.

Research: Recovery + Employment Evidence

IPS Model

Strongest evidence-based employment model for recovery

Critical Integration Point

Many families in recovery receive Medicaid, SNAP, and disability benefits. Without benefits cliff navigation, a new job can trigger benefit loss at the worst possible moment. Losing healthcare or housing support during early recovery can trigger relapse. Individual Placement and Support (IPS) is the strongest evidence-based employment model for this work.

Key Insight: Recovery programs stabilize. Empower provides the "what comes next." In Medicaid expansion states, uninsured rates for opioid-related hospitalizations dropped from 13.4% to 2.9%. Healthcare access during recovery isn't optional. It's the difference between relapse and a livable-wage career.

Justice-Involved

Re-Entry Programs (Returning Citizens)

Download Re-Entry Guide

Returning citizens face every standard barrier plus more: criminal records, supervision requirements, work history gaps, employer hesitancy. The Empower Method gives navigators a structure for this layered navigation.

Why the Navigator Model is Critical: These families have been failed by institutions over and over. Trust takes time. One navigator. 18-24 months. No handoffs. Consistency is everything.

Key Insight: Certified employers matter most here. They understand background check navigation and commit to fair-chance hiring. Without them, returning citizens hit a wall.

11.2 Education System Adaptations

Here's the breakthrough: the same barriers that trap families in poverty are the same barriers that prevent children from graduating. When families can't pay rent, kids miss school. When parents lose childcare, teenagers drop out to babysit. The Empower Method transforms educational outcomes by wrapping support around the whole family. Not just the student.

K-12 Education

Student Success & Graduation Navigator

Download K-12 Guide

The Hidden Connection

Family economic instability is the #1 predictor of dropout risk. Not grades. Not test scores. When parents face eviction, lose childcare, or juggle three jobs, kids miss school. They can't focus. They disengage. Fix the family's economic barriers and you fix student success.

How the Four Non-Negotiables Apply to K-12:

1

Benefits Cliff Navigation → Family Stability Planning

When families hit benefits cliffs, students hit attendance cliffs. Navigate income changes for parents so kids don't become collateral damage. Partner with workforce agencies to drive parent employment.

2

Navigator Model → Family Success Coach

One Family Success Coach per 30-40 families. Same person for 4+ years. Elementary through middle school. Middle through high school. Builds trust that guidance counselors rotating every semester never will.

3

Wraparound Coordination → Community Schools Model

Coordinate every service toward family economic stability. Housing. Food. Healthcare. Childcare. Transportation. All pointed at one target: parents on a path to livable-wage careers.

4

Data Dashboard → Early Warning System

Track attendance patterns, grade trends, behavior incidents, and family stability indicators. Predict dropout risk 2-3 years out. Intervene before the crisis hits.

Families with navigator support produce students 2.5x more likely to enroll in post-secondary education. Address family economic barriers and you don't just improve graduation rates. You build the pipeline to college and livable-wage careers.

Ages 0-5

Early Childhood & Head Start

Download Early Childhood Guide

Head Start already thinks in two generations. The Empower Method gives that philosophy real economic mobility infrastructure. Parent services stop being supplemental. They become transformational.

The Childcare Cliff Crisis

Childcare subsidies have some of the steepest benefits cliffs in the system. A $0.50/hour raise can cost a family $8,000+ in childcare. Early childhood programs sit right at the cliff's edge. They can help families navigate it BEFORE childcare disappears.

Key Insight: Stabilize a family during ages 0-5 and you set the trajectory for K-12 success. Early intervention delivers the highest ROI of any education investment. Period.

Veterans

Download Veterans Guide

Veterans transitioning to civilian life face distinct barriers: translating military skills, navigating VA benefits, managing service-connected conditions while building careers. The navigator model respects military culture and provides the civilian-side navigation these families need.

Key Insight: Veterans often qualify for benefits they don't know exist. And they face brutal cliffs when VA disability interacts with employment income. Benefits cliff navigation isn't optional here. It's essential.

11.4 Workplace Adaptation: Supervisor-as-Navigator

Here's what most people miss: the Empower Method doesn't require a social service agency. Any employer with ALICE workers can adopt these principles. Supervisors become workplace navigators.

The Business Case

Replacing one hourly employee costs $3,500-$5,000. If benefits cliffs push 20% of your ALICE workforce to quit or refuse promotions, that's a massive hidden cost. Supervisor-navigator training pays for itself in reduced turnover. Fast.

11.5 International Adaptation: Canada and Beyond

Benefits cliffs exist everywhere social safety nets exist. The Four Non-Negotiables translate across borders. What changes is the language, agency names, and specific benefit structures. The core approach stays the same.

International

Canada: Provincial Benefits, Federal Opportunity

Download Canada Adaptation Guide

Why Canada First?

Circles Canada already operates in Toronto and other communities. The infrastructure exists. What's missing is the economic navigation layer. That's exactly what Empower delivers.

Same Problems, Different Names
  • • Benefits cliffs (different labels)
  • • Fragmented benefit programs
  • • Provincial/federal split
  • • Workforce boards in place
  • • Human services agencies
Canadian Translation
  • • Ontario Works = TANF equivalent
  • • Disability Support Program = state disability
  • • Provincial social services ministries
  • • Employment Ontario = workforce system
  • • Stronger healthcare coverage baseline
Why the Timing Works
  • • Federal poverty alleviation focus
  • • Government funding priorities align
  • • Smaller geography = faster scaling
  • • Fewer competing models
  • • First-mover advantage
Critical Research Question

The US has the Atlanta Fed's CLIFF Dashboard. It maps exactly where benefits cliffs hit in every state. It's public and free.

Question for Canadian adaptation: Does Canada have an equivalent tool mapping provincial benefits cliffs? If not, that's an opportunity. Partner with Canadian research institutions to build it first.

The Market Insight

"Get in the door with Canadian communities now. When federal poverty funding flows, you capture market share that's impossible in the fragmented US system. A proven US model. A Canadian playbook. First-mover wins."

How to Adapt for Any Country

1
Map Local Benefit Structures

Map every social safety net program: eligibility thresholds, phase-out rates, cliff points. Learn the local terminology. Benefits have different names everywhere. The cliffs work the same.

2
Find the Local Equivalents

Workforce development, social services, housing, healthcare. Every country has them. They're just named differently. Find them. Build relationships with these anchor partners.

3
Swap the Labels, Keep the Principles

Ontario Works instead of TANF. Different name, same design problem. Keep the Four Non-Negotiables intact. The navigation approach is universal. Only the program names change.

4
Build on What Exists

Find local organizations already doing wraparound work (like Circles Canada). Partner with them. Layer Empower's navigation precision on top of their infrastructure.

The proof point is simple. If this model works with TANF families in rural Tennessee, it works anywhere families face benefits cliffs and fragmented services. Benefits cliff navigation. Navigator relationships. Wraparound coordination. Data-driven decisions. These aren't American ideas. They're universal truths about how poverty works.

11.6 What Stays the Same vs. What Adapts

Non-Negotiable (Never Changes)

  • ✓ Benefits cliff navigation — Model before every income change
  • ✓ Sustained relationship — One navigator stays through the whole journey
  • ✓ Whole-family view — Address interconnected barriers together
  • ✓ Data-driven decisions — Track outcomes, not activities
  • ✓ Self-sufficiency target — 225% FPL / ALICE threshold
  • ✓ Outcomes over outputs — Measure impact, not referral counts

Adaptable (Context-Specific)

  • • Who serves as navigator — Case manager, supervisor, teacher, CHW
  • • Timeline — 18-24 months standard. Longer for education and recovery.
  • • Specific benefits tracked — Varies by state and population
  • • Wraparound partners — Different agencies for every context
  • • Primary outcomes — Graduation, employment, housing, recovery
  • • Soft skills curriculum — Tailored to sector and population
Key Takeaway

The Empower Method was built to adapt. The Four Non-Negotiables are the DNA. They don't change. Everything else flexes to context:

✓ HRAs — Connect existing services into one system
✓ Schools — Graduation and college pipelines
✓ Recovery — Economic path after stabilization
✓ Healthcare — Attack root causes, not symptoms
✓ Re-entry — Multi-barrier navigation for returning citizens
✓ Employers — Supervisor-navigators cut turnover
✓ Canada & Beyond — Benefits cliffs are universal

Same DNA. Different context. That's how one model becomes a movement.

11.7 Tennessee Implementation

Tennessee is a distinct environment. No Medicaid expansion. Strict TANF time limits. A strong Development District network. Organizations implementing Empower here need Tennessee-specific guidance. This section delivers it.

TennCare & Healthcare Navigation

Tennessee runs TennCare, not traditional Medicaid. Navigators need to know these distinctions:

Coverage Gaps

Adults 19-64 without dependents generally don't qualify unless disabled. This creates a gap: too much income for TennCare, too little for marketplace subsidies. Navigators must find alternatives. Community health centers. Sliding-scale clinics. Employer coverage pathways.

TennCare Connect

Eligibility runs through TennCare Connect (tenncare.tn.gov). Processing takes 30-45 days. Submit applications early. Have interim healthcare plans ready for families in the gap.

CoverKids

Children's coverage reaches 250% FPL through CoverKids. That's ABOVE the ALICE threshold. Kids keep coverage while parents increase income. This matters for benefits cliff planning.

Families First (TANF) Time Limits

Tennessee's TANF program (Families First) has strict time limits. Navigators must plan around them:

60 months

Lifetime federal limit

18 months

Continuous receipt limit (requires 3-month break)

Navigator Implication

Many families have already burned through much of their lifetime limit. Navigators must check remaining TANF months on day one. Build plans that don't assume continued TANF. The 18-month continuous limit means families cycle on and off. Track carefully or benefit termination blindsides everyone.

Work Participation Requirements

Tennessee requires 35 hours/week of work activities for single parents (55 for two-parent households). Education alone doesn't count. It must combine with work. Navigators help families structure schedules that meet requirements while pursuing credentials.

Tennessee-Specific Benefits Cliffs

Child Care Payment Assistance (CCPA)

Eligible up to 85% State Median Income. Tennessee uses a co-pay structure that rises with income. This is a critical cliff to map. Waiting lists vary by county. Enroll families early.

SNAP (TN)

Tennessee has NOT adopted broad-based categorical eligibility. Stricter income limits than many states. Gross income capped at 130% FPL. ABAWD work requirements apply in most counties.

LIHEAP & Utility Assistance

Low Income Home Energy Assistance runs through HRAs. Seasonal programs. Fall applications for winter heating. Spring for summer cooling. Build this into annual planning cycles.
