Non-Negotiable #1: Benefits Cliff Navigation

What It Is

The system punishes effort. A family earns a raise, and the system yanks their benefits. Benefits cliff navigation stops that trap before it springs. We model every benefit transition before families accept raises or new jobs. Not "I just lost my childcare subsidy!" but "If you take this raise, you'll lose $6,800--here's the plan."

Why It's Non-Negotiable

The benefits cliff is the #1 reason working families stay trapped in poverty. Without navigation:

  • A $2/hour raise costs a family $6,800 in childcare subsidies. Hard work literally makes them poorer.
  • Employers promote their best people--and accidentally devastate their finances
  • Career advancement becomes punishment. The system designed it that way.

The Fear Is Just as Destructive as the Cliff Itself

Families turn down promotions and extra hours because they've been burned before--or watched someone else get burned. Even when the math works in their favor, fear keeps them stuck. That's stranded talent. Benefits cliff navigation doesn't just dodge real cliffs. It shows families when it's safe to climb and when to wait.

No cliff navigation, no self-sufficiency. The math doesn't lie.

How It Works

How We Navigate the Cliff

1

Map Current Benefits

Document every benefit the family receives. SNAP, Medicaid/TennCare, childcare subsidy, housing assistance, TANF--all of it. No surprises later.

2

Identify Cliff Points

Pinpoint exactly where each benefit drops off. Build a visual map of every danger zone. Families see the traps before they step into them.

3

Model Scenarios

Run the numbers before any decision. "This $3/hour raise costs you $4,200 in childcare subsidy." Families decide with full information, not blind hope.

4

Create Transition Plan

Sequence income increases strategically. Sometimes: take a smaller raise now, stack savings, then leap past multiple cliffs at once.

5

Coordinate with Employer

Partner with certified employers to time raises, restructure compensation, or provide benefits that offset cliff losses. Employers become part of the solution.

Minimum Requirement

A benefits cliff calculator and trained staff who know how to use it. Every family gets their personal cliff map before making any job or income decision. No exceptions.

Non-Negotiable #2: The Navigator Model

What It Is

One person. One family. 18-24 months. The full journey. Navigators don't hand off. They don't rotate. They don't refer and disappear. They stay.

Why It's Non-Negotiable

Trust takes time. Changing someone's economic trajectory takes longer.

Families have been failed by every system that promised to help. They don't open up in the first meeting. They test you. They wait for you to disappear like everyone else did. The relationship IS the intervention.

Trust Changes Everything

Research shows trust-based relationships produce an "amplifying effect" on every outcome. When families trust their navigator:

  • They share the real barriers--not just the safe ones
  • They push through hard steps because someone has their back
  • They survive setbacks without giving up--the relationship holds them steady
  • They start advocating for themselves because they've watched their navigator do it

Moving from crisis to stability to self-sufficiency is not a 90-day process. It takes 18-24 months to:

  • Put out the fires--housing, childcare, transportation
  • Navigate the benefits cliff strategically
  • Build skills and land livable-wage careers
  • Survive setbacks without spiraling back to square one
  • Cross the ALICE threshold into true self-sufficiency

18-24 Months. Not Optional.

Research is clear: 18-24 months is the minimum for lasting economic mobility. Shorter programs don't just fail to help--relationships under 6 months can produce negative outcomes by creating dependency without building capacity. Quick fixes create more damage. We don't do quick fixes.

Minimum Requirement

A dedicated navigator role. Not split with case management. Not shared across programs. If your "navigators" are also handling rotating caseloads, you don't have navigators. You have overloaded case managers with a new title. That's not the same thing.

Non-Negotiable #3: Wraparound Coordination

What It Is

We clear what stands in families' way. Housing, childcare, transportation, healthcare, financial coaching--all coordinated, all pointed at one goal: a livable-wage career. Not a scattered checklist of services. A unified attack on every barrier between a family and financial independence.

One Question Drives Everything

Every service we coordinate answers one question: "What's standing between this family and a livable-wage career?"

  • Unstable housing? → Fix it so they can show up to work every day
  • No childcare? → Fix it so they can accept full-time hours
  • Car broke down? → Fix it so they can reach the job site
  • Health crisis? → Fix it so they don't lose shifts or focus

The wraparound is not the goal. The livable-wage career is the goal. Wraparound clears the path.

Why It's Non-Negotiable

Families can't hold a livable-wage career when their life is collapsing around them. And barriers don't wait politely in line:

  • Land a great job, but childcare falls through. Job gone.
  • Get stable housing, but the car dies. Can't get to work. Housing gone.
  • Accept a promotion, but lose Medicaid. One medical bill. Back to square one.

Livable-wage careers require stability across multiple domains at the same time. We coordinate services not to check boxes, but to clear everything standing in a family's way.

Non-Negotiable #4: Data Dashboard

What It Is

One system. Unified intake, real-time tracking, and outcomes data--visible to every team member. Navigators see what they need in real time. Leadership sees patterns for strategic decisions. No silos. No guessing.

Why It's Non-Negotiable

You can't manage what you can't see. You can't coordinate what you can't share.

Without unified data:

  • Navigators have no idea what other agencies are doing with their families
  • Leadership flies blind--no patterns, no course corrections
  • Wins get lost. Failures repeat.
  • Funders can't see ROI. Funding dries up.

What the Dashboard Tracks

Intake Data
  • Family composition
  • Current income
  • Benefits received
  • Barriers identified
  • Goals set
Tracking Data
  • Navigator interactions
  • Services accessed
  • Progress toward goals
  • Barriers encountered
  • Benefits cliff status
Outcomes Data
  • Income changes
  • Employment status
  • Benefits transitions
  • ALICE threshold progress
  • Graduation status

Key Principle

Data for decisions. Not just compliance.

The dashboard is not a quarterly report for funders (though it handles that, too). It's a daily tool. Navigators use it to make real-time decisions. Leadership uses it to spot patterns and course-correct fast.

Minimum Requirement

A central tracking system every team member can access. Start simple if you need to--a well-structured spreadsheet works. But it must be unified and shared. Siloed data kills coordination.
