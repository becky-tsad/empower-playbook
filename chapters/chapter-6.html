The Certified Employer Program

How employers become active partners in the self-sufficiency journey

Most workforce programs end at job placement. The Empower Method makes employers active partners in the self-sufficiency journey. The Poverty Solution team has developed a Certified Employer Program that goes beyond training—employers complete Poverty IQ education, commit to competitive compensation, coordinate raises through benefits cliff tools, and partner with navigators on long-term retention. Certification means an employer is truly equipped to hire, support, and advance families moving out of poverty.

Why This Matters

Job placement without employer preparation creates revolving doors. Employers who don't understand ALICE populations—the benefits cliff, the barriers, the reality of working poverty—accidentally harm the families they're trying to help. A well-meaning raise can cost a family thousands. A rigid attendance policy can punish someone whose childcare falls through.

Certified employers don't just hire participants. They're equipped to retain and advance them.

Job Placement Does Not Equal Job Retention

Here's a statistic that haunts traditional workforce programs: within 6 months, most job placements fail. Participants are back where they started—or worse, now with a gap in their resume.

Why? Because the employer side of the equation was never prepared:

  • Supervisors don't understand why a stellar employee calls out when childcare falls through
  • HR gives a raise without realizing it will cost the employee more than they gain
  • The culture punishes the "messy" realities of working while poor

Two-Way Accountability

The Empower Method creates accountability on both sides of the employment relationship:

Participant Responsibility

  • Complete soft skills training
  • Communicate proactively about barriers
  • Engage with navigator and financial coaching
  • Work toward skill development

Employer Responsibility

  • Complete Poverty IQ training
  • Coordinate raises through benefits cliff tool
  • Provide reasonable flexibility
  • Partner on retention, not just hire

6.2 Certified Employer Requirements

To become a Certified Employer in the Empower network, a business must complete the following:

Certification Checklist

1

Poverty IQ Training

Complete training on what it's actually like to hire someone navigating poverty. Covers: benefits cliffs, transportation barriers, childcare realities, financial instability, and how these affect work performance.

Duration: ~2 hours | Required for: HR + direct supervisors

2

ALICE Orientation

Basic education on the ALICE population—Asset Limited, Income Constrained, Employed. Understanding that your future employees are already working, already trying, and trapped by systems—not personal failures.

Duration: 30 minutes | Can be video-based

3

Benefits Cliff Tool Access

Agree to coordinate raises and promotions through the benefits cliff tool. Before offering a raise, HR contacts the navigator to model the impact. Raises are structured to help, not hurt.

Ongoing commitment | HR point of contact required

4

Job Slot Commitment

Commit to reserving X positions for pilot participants. This can be "as positions open" or a specific number per quarter. The key: participants have a pipeline, not just a job board.

Flexible based on employer size

5

Retention Partnership

Agree to work with the pilot to retain employees—not just hire and forget. This means: communicating issues early, coordinating with navigators when problems arise, giving participants a fair chance to address barriers.

Ongoing commitment | Regular check-ins with employer liaison

6

Competitive Compensation

Offer livable wages and benefits where possible. Ideal: healthcare, paid time off, predictable schedules. Minimum: wages that move families toward—not away from—self-sufficiency.

Context-dependent | Not all employers can offer full benefits

Job Quality Standards

One critical component is how you define a "good job" for your community. The goal is family-sustaining employment—but what that means varies by local cost of living, industry mix, and family composition.

Minimum Job Quality Indicators

Compensation

  • Wage meets local ALICE survival threshold
  • Consistent hours (30+ weekly minimum)
  • Predictable scheduling

Benefits Access

  • Health insurance offered (employer or ACA)
  • Paid sick leave available
  • Retirement savings option

Advancement Pathway

  • Clear path to wage increases
  • Skill development opportunities
  • Internal promotion history

Work-Life Compatibility

  • Schedule accommodates childcare
  • Commute under 45 minutes
  • Emergency flexibility policies

Certified Employers should meet most of these criteria. Navigators should counsel families toward employers who do—even if it means waiting for a better opportunity rather than taking the first available job.

6.3 The Two-Way Pipeline

Certified employers don't just post jobs and wait for applications. They participate in a coordinated pipeline that matches trained participants to specific opportunities.

How the Pipeline Works

Employer

"I have 5 warehouse positions opening in 3 months"

➔

Pilot

Receives advance notice of opportunity

Pilot

Identifies participants ready for warehouse work

➔

Navigator

Begins targeted soft skills training

Navigator

Coordinates childcare, transportation, benefits cliff

➔

Participant

Arrives job-ready with support in place

Participant

Starts job with navigator support continuing

➔

Employer

Gets retention support, coordinates raises

This is the opposite of spray-and-pray job placement. It's a coordinated system where:

  • Employers get pre-screened, prepared candidates with support structures in place
  • Participants get real opportunities they're trained for, not random job postings
  • Navigators can prepare wraparound support before issues arise

6.4 Building Your Employer Network

Proven Results: Employer Support Programs

UPS Stability Support Pilot

When UPS provided stability support services to entry-level workers, 90-day retention jumped from 69% to 96%—a transformation that dramatically reduced recruitment and training costs.

Employer Childcare Support

Companies offering childcare support document an average 425% ROI through reduced turnover and absenteeism.

Benefits Cliff Awareness in Ohio

1 in 5 Ohio businesses reported experiencing personnel issues related to benefits cliff concerns—employees declining raises or promotions to preserve benefits.

Note: Recovery-friendly workplace certifications now exist nationally, providing a model for employer certification programs focused on specific workforce challenges.

Start Small: 3-5 Anchor Employers

You don't need every employer in your region certified on day one. Start with 3-5 anchor employers who:

  • Are already community-minded (they show up at chamber meetings, sponsor local events)
  • Have positions that pay livable wages
  • Have HR capacity to coordinate with your team
  • Are willing to be early adopters and help refine the process

Expand Through Employer Referrals

Once your anchor employers see results—better retention, less turnover, more reliable employees—they become your best recruiters. "This program sent me three great hires" is more compelling than any pitch deck.

Celebrate Certified Employers Publicly

Make certification visible:

  • Window decal/badge: "Empower Certified Employer"
  • Website listing: Public directory of certified employers
  • Press recognition: Announce new certifications in local media
  • Annual awards: Recognize employers with exceptional retention

Certification becomes a mark of community investment—and a competitive advantage for employers trying to attract workers who want more than just a job.

Key Takeaway

A Certified Employer is a business that:

  • Completes Poverty IQ training
  • Receives ALICE orientation
  • Coordinates raises through the benefits cliff tool
  • Commits job slots to program participants
  • Partners on retention, not just placement
  • Offers competitive compensation

This is the missing piece in most workforce programs—and one of the biggest differentiators of the Empower Method.
