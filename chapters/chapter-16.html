The Competitive Landscape

How Empower stacks up against other economic mobility models -- and why our approach fills a gap nobody else is filling for rural communities.

The economic mobility field has exploded over the past two decades. Coaching models. Peer networks. Cash transfers. Benefits cliff tools. There's real innovation happening. But understanding where Empower fits -- and what it does that others don't -- matters for anyone considering implementation.

16.1 Major Economic Mobility Models

The field has strong models. Each one does something well. Knowing their strengths makes it clear where Empower fills the gaps.

Mobility Mentoring

EMPath (Economic Mobility Pathways)

Visit Website

EMPath pioneered Mobility Mentoring -- using brain science and economic frameworks to help families build stability across five domains.

Model Highlights
  • * 5-domain assessment (financial, education, employment, health, housing)
  • * Brain science-informed coaching
  • * Executive function skill-building
  • * Mobility Mentoring licensed nationally
Reported Outcomes
  • * 97% employed at program exit
  • * $32,548 average income at exit
  • * 25:1 caseload early, 60:1 later phases
  • * 18-24 month program duration
Gap Analysis
  • * No benefits cliff focus as the entry point
  • * Built for urban communities
  • * Licensed model requires significant upfront training investment
Coaching + Cash

LIFT

Visit Website

LIFT pairs goal-based coaching with direct cash transfers to help parents build stability and break the cycle of intergenerational poverty.

Model Highlights
  • * Parent-focused (2-generation approach)
  • * $150/quarter direct cash transfers
  • * 2-year program duration
  • * Monthly contact with coaches
Reported Outcomes
  • * $21,325 average income increase
  • * 94% report improved financial situation
  • * 73% achieve primary goal
  • * Strong parent satisfaction scores
Gap Analysis
  • * Monthly contact only -- not intensive navigation
  • * No benefits cliff expertise
  • * Urban only: Chicago, DC, LA, NYC
Peer Volunteer Model

Circles USA

Visit Website

Circles builds intentional cross-class relationships. Families working to increase income ("Circle Leaders") connect with middle-income volunteers ("Allies") for support and accountability.

Model Highlights
  • * Cross-class relationship building
  • * Weekly community meetings
  • * Peer support + volunteer "Allies"
  • * 18-month minimum commitment
Reported Outcomes
  • * 71% income increase at 18 months
  • * Strong social capital gains
  • * Community cohesion benefits
  • * 80+ communities nationwide
Gap Analysis
  • * Volunteers, not professional navigators
  • * No specialized benefits cliff knowledge
  • * Depends on steady volunteer recruitment
Trust-Based Investment

Family Independence Initiative (UpTogether)

Visit Website

UpTogether (formerly FII) trusts families to drive their own solutions. Unrestricted cash. Peer support. Tracking tools. No prescribed interventions or case management.

Model Highlights
  • * Unrestricted cash transfers
  • * Self-determination philosophy
  • * Peer groups (no professional staff)
  • * Digital platform for tracking
Reported Outcomes
  • * $200M+ distributed to 200,000+ households
  • * Significant income and savings gains
  • * Strong evidence for cash transfers
  • * High family satisfaction
Gap Analysis
  • * Peer-only -- no professional navigation
  • * No benefits cliff expertise or modeling
  • * Cash alone can't solve cliff barriers
Benefits Cliff Tool

Atlanta Fed CLIFF Tools

Visit Website

The Atlanta Fed built the CLIFF (Career Ladder Identifier and Financial Forecaster) tools so workforce professionals and families can see exactly how income changes affect benefits.

Model Highlights
  • * Benefits cliff modeling calculators
  • * State-specific cliff data
  • * Free public tools
  • * Integration with workforce systems
Reported Outcomes
  • * 90% of users made action plans after coaching with tools
  • * Widely adopted by workforce boards
  • * Growing national awareness of cliffs
  • * Policy advocacy impact
Gap Analysis
  • * Tool only -- no integrated program model
  • * No navigator relationship
  • * You still need separate infrastructure to act on the data

16.3 The Five-Part Convergence

No single element makes Empower unique. It's the convergence of five elements that other programs treat as separate. Here's why each matters -- and why combining them changes the game.

1

Benefits Cliff as ENTRY POINT (Not Afterthought)

Most programs discover cliff barriers after a placement fails. Empower starts with cliff analysis -- mapping every benefit, modeling income scenarios, planning transitions BEFORE making employment decisions.

Why it matters: A $2/hour raise that triggers $8,000 in childcare loss isn't a win. It's a trap. Starting with cliffs prevents "successful" placements that actually hurt families.

2

Professional Navigators (Not Peer Volunteers)

Peer models build social capital. Professional navigators build systems knowledge. ALICE families facing cliff barriers need someone who understands Medicaid redetermination timing, childcare subsidy calculations, and employer benefit negotiation.

Why it matters: Benefits navigation requires technical expertise. A caring volunteer who doesn't understand 12-month lookback periods can give advice that costs a family thousands.

3

Wraparound for Economic Mobility (Not Just Crisis or Child Welfare)

Traditional wraparound targets child welfare or behavioral health crises. Empower applies that same coordination intensity to economic mobility. Because poverty is a system. You have to treat it like one.

Why it matters: Transportation connects to childcare connects to employment connects to benefits. You can't solve one piece in isolation. Economic mobility deserves the same systems coordination as crisis intervention.

4

Real-Time Data Dashboard (Not Retrospective)

Most programs report outcomes once a year. Empower tracks them in real-time. Navigators see when a family approaches a cliff. When engagement drops. When intervention is needed NOW.

Why it matters: Finding out a family fell off a cliff six months ago doesn't help anyone. Real-time data lets you intervene before a crisis becomes a catastrophe.

5

Rural-Specific Design (Not Urban-Adapted)

Most economic mobility programs were built in cities and "adapted" to rural areas. Empower was built IN rural Tennessee, FOR rural communities. Transportation deserts. Service scarcity. The relationship-based trust that rural culture demands.

Why it matters: Urban solutions assume buses, multiple service providers, and anonymity. Rural communities run on personal relationships, long distances, and limited options. Different context. Different design.

The field has strong models. But none are built specifically for rural families facing benefits cliff barriers. Empower doesn't replace them. It fills the gap they leave:

* EMPath provides coaching frameworks—Empower adds cliff expertise
* LIFT provides cash + coaching—Empower adds intensive navigation
* Circles builds community—Empower adds professional expertise
* UpTogether trusts families—Empower adds systems knowledge
* CLIFF Tools models cliffs—Empower adds full program infrastructure

Rural families need a model built for them. Not adapted to them. That's Empower.
