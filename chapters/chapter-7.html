The Phased Approach

Build the system right. Then bring families in.

Most programs launch fast and fail faster. The Empower Method flips that. You build the infrastructure first. Then you bring families in. When they walk through the door, the system works. Not someday. Day one.

Why Phasing Matters: The Research Is Clear

Programs that rush to scale deliver weaker results in the real world than they did in controlled trials. Phasing protects outcomes as you grow.

Proof It Works: Nurse-Family Partnership

The Nurse-Family Partnership pulled off something almost no one does: lower costs per family at scale ($8,860) than in original trials ($12,398). How? Disciplined phased implementation. They built the infrastructure right before they expanded. We do the same.

Non-Negotiable

Do NOT enroll families in the first 6 months. Zero. This phase builds the foundation. Rushing families into a half-built system creates the exact fragmentation you set out to fix.

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 0-6)

Goal: Build the infrastructure. No families yet.

Get your house in order. Align the vision. Hire the right people. Train them. Lock in partnerships. Stand up the systems. Skip this phase and you will spend years cleaning up the mess.

Phase 1 Checklist

How You Know Phase 1 Is Done

Move to Phase 2 when every box is checked:

  • Shared vision document signed by all partners
  • All staff hired, trained, and certified
  • Dashboard live and tested
  • 3-5 employers in the certification pipeline (at least 2 certified)
  • Partner agreements signed and filed
  • Every team member can explain the Four Non-Negotiables without hesitation

Phase 2: Activation (Months 6-12)

Goal: Serve families. Prove the model works.

Now you bring families in. Start small. This is still a learning phase. You are testing processes, finding friction, and refining before you scale.

Phase 2 Checklist

How You Know Phase 2 Is Working

  • X families enrolled with complete intake data
  • X employers certified
  • First job placements documented and tracked
  • Coordination rhythm running on schedule
  • Zero families lost to fragmentation — every person has an active navigator
  • Early income gains showing up in the dashboard

Phase 3: Momentum (Months 12-24)

Goal: Scale what works. Fix what does not.

By now you know what works in your community. Phase 3 scales the model without sacrificing quality. This is also when you see graduates — families who have crossed the ALICE threshold and no longer need the system to hold them up.

Phase 3 Checklist

How You Know Phase 3 Is Working

  • Families graduating above the ALICE threshold
  • The system runs without daily firefighting
  • ROI documented and in funders' hands
  • Employers on a waiting list for certification
  • Real transformation stories documented
  • 12+ months of funding runway secured

The Minimum Viable Implementation

Not every community starts with full funding. We know that. Here is how to right-size the launch:

Starter

  • 1 Navigator
  • Benefits cliff tool
  • Basic tracking (spreadsheet OK)
  • Serve 25-50 families

Growth

  • 2-3 Navigators
  • Full dashboard
  • 5+ certified employers
  • Serve 100-200 families

Full Model

  • Full team (see Ch. 8)
  • All Four Non-Negotiables
  • 15+ certified employers
  • Serve 500+ families

The rule: Start where you can. But never compromise the Four Non-Negotiables. A small implementation with all four beats a large one missing even one.

Key Takeaway

The phased approach protects families and your team:

  • Phase 1 (0-6 mo): Build infrastructure. No families yet.
  • Phase 2 (6-12 mo): Small cohort. Learn. Refine.
  • Phase 3 (12-24 mo): Scale what works. Graduate families.

Rushing families into a broken system creates the fragmentation you set out to fix. Build it right. Then open the doors.
