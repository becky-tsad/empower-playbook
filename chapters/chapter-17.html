Scaling with Fidelity

Most programs fail at scale. The Nurse-Family Partnership didn't. Here's how Empower's Fidelity Framework keeps the model working as it grows.

Good programs don't fail because they stop working. They fail because they drift. As they scale, they quietly abandon the core elements that produced results. This chapter draws on 45 years of Nurse-Family Partnership data and implementation science to build Empower's framework for scaling without losing what works.

The Fidelity Paradox

Too much local adaptation kills effectiveness. Too little kills adoption. The answer: define what's non-negotiable (Core Model Elements) and what's adaptable (local implementation choices). Then hold the line.

Source: Durlak & DuPre (2008), "Implementation Matters: A Review of Research on the Influence of Implementation on Program Outcomes"

17.1 Why Programs Fail at Scale

The research is sobering. Most evidence-based programs lose effectiveness when they scale beyond the original pilot. Knowing why it happens is step one to preventing it.

The Scale-Up Problem by the Numbers

52%

of nonprofits modify programs to fit available funding

Source: Nonprofit Finance Fund Survey, 2018

40-60%

reduction in effect sizes in real-world vs. controlled settings

Source: Weisz et al., "Promoting and Protecting Youth Mental Health"

Five Root Causes of Program Drift

Why Scaling Fails

1

Mission Drift from Funding Pressure

Programs reshape themselves around what funders will pay for instead of what works. "We'll just skip the home visits to fit the budget." Then it becomes permanent.

2

Staff Turnover Destroys Knowledge

Experienced staff leave. Nothing's documented. Each new hire makes different decisions. The "why" behind every practice disappears.

3

No Standardization

No written operations guide means every site runs on interpretation. "What we think the model is" slowly diverges from "what the model actually is."

4

Death by a Thousand Cuts

Caseloads creep from 1:25 to 1:40 to 1:60. Visits drop. Training shortens. Each change seems small. The cumulative effect is catastrophic.

5

Untested "Improvements"

Local sites make small tweaks they believe are better. Without evaluation, harmful changes look like good ideas -- until outcomes collapse.

"The gap between knowledge about effective programs and knowledge about how to implement them successfully is perhaps the most significant issue facing prevention science."
— Fixsen et al., Implementation Research: A Synthesis of the Literature (2005)

17.2 The NFP Model - Gold Standard for Scaling

The Nurse-Family Partnership (NFP) is one of the few social programs that scaled while maintaining effectiveness -- and even improving it. 45 years of data. 300,000+ families served annually. NFP proves it can be done.

17.3 Empower's Fidelity Framework

Empower's Fidelity Framework borrows from NFP and implementation science. It draws a hard line between Core Model Elements (non-negotiable) and Adaptable Elements (your call locally).

Core Model Elements (Non-Negotiable)

These eight elements are the active ingredients. Weaken any of them and the model stops working.

Empower Core Model Elements

1

Benefits Cliff Assessment Before Employment Planning

Every family gets a full benefits cliff analysis BEFORE any employment planning begins. No exceptions.

2

Single Navigator Relationship for 18-24 Months Minimum

Same navigator, start to finish. No handoffs. No reassignments for convenience. The relationship IS the model.

3

1:25 or Lower Caseload Ratio for Intensive Phase

During the first 12 months, no navigator carries more than 25 intensive cases. The depth of the relationship produces the outcomes.

4

Wraparound Coordination with Documented Partner MOUs

Formal MOUs with partners define referral pathways, data sharing, and coordination. Informal "relationships" aren't enough.

5

Real-Time Data Dashboard Tracking Outcomes

Not annual reports. Real-time. Leaders and navigators see current outcomes weekly. Problems get caught fast.

6

225% FPL / ALICE Threshold as Self-Sufficiency Target

Families graduate when they hit economic stability. Not when funding runs out. Not when an arbitrary timeline expires.

7

Employer Certification with Cliff Awareness Training

Employers hiring families in the program must complete Poverty IQ training. The workplace has to support progress, not undermine it.

8

Monthly Case Review with Supervisor

Every active case reviewed monthly with a supervisor. Prevents burnout. Catches drift early. Keeps quality consistent.

Adaptable Elements (Local Choice)

These elements should flex to fit local context, population, and resources. Adapting them doesn't compromise fidelity -- it strengthens fit.

Adaptable Elements

Specific Benefits Tracked

Varies by state. Some have stronger SNAP. Others have better childcare subsidies. Match the cliff analysis to the local benefit landscape.

Partner Organizations

Every community has different organizations. The requirement is formal MOUs. Which partners you choose is up to you.

Soft Skills Curriculum Content

Tailor it to your population -- youth vs. adults, recovery vs. re-entry. The requirement is that soft skills development happens. The content is your call.

Timeline Beyond 18 Months

18 months is the floor. Extend to 24, 36, or longer based on what families need and funding allows.

Primary Outcome Emphasis

Livable-wage careers, graduation, housing stability, recovery milestones -- the primary target varies by program focus.

Communication Methods

Text, app, phone, in-person. Whatever works for families, as long as contact frequency stays consistent.

17.5 Quality Assurance Infrastructure

Fidelity doesn't happen by accident. It takes deliberate infrastructure: training, supervision, monitoring, and continuous improvement.

Navigator Certification Pathway

  1. 1

    Pre-Service Training (40 hours)

    Before carrying any cases

  2. 2

    Supervised Practice (3 months)

    Reduced caseload with intensive supervision

  3. 3

    Competency Assessment

    Observed skills demonstration

  4. 4

    Certification

    Annual renewal with continuing education

Training Curriculum Requirements

  • Benefits cliff modeling (8 hours)
  • Motivational interviewing (16 hours)
  • Trauma-informed practice (8 hours)
  • Local benefits landscape (4 hours)
  • Data system proficiency (4 hours)

Supervision Protocols

  • Weekly team meetings (group supervision)
  • Monthly individual supervision (1:1)
  • Quarterly field observation
  • Annual performance review with competency assessment

Data Systems for Monitoring

  • Real-time outcome dashboard
  • Caseload monitoring alerts
  • Contact frequency tracking
  • Referral completion rates
  • Fidelity metrics by site

The NFP Lesson: Coaching Paired with Feedback

NFP proved that training alone doesn't keep fidelity. You need real-time performance data + coaching support together. That's what prevents drift.

When supervisors see a navigator's contact frequency dropping, they intervene immediately. Before outcomes suffer. Data without coaching is surveillance. Coaching without data is guessing.

Source: Fixsen et al. (2009), "Core Implementation Components"

17.6 Warning Signs of Model Drift

Drift never announces itself. It creeps in through small compromises that seem reasonable in the moment. Train your leaders to spot these warning signs.

Early Warning Signs of Model Drift

Caseloads creeping above 1:30

"Just temporarily -- 32 cases until we hire someone." Temporary becomes permanent. The 1:25 ratio exists because the research says so.

Benefits cliff assessment skipped "to save time"

"They just need a job -- we'll do the cliff analysis later." Later never comes. Families hit cliffs that could have been avoided.

Navigator reassignments breaking relationships

"It's more efficient to specialize navigators." Efficiency kills the relationship. The entire model depends on continuity.

Dashboard data not reviewed regularly

"We're too busy serving families to look at data." You can't know if you're succeeding without looking. Flying blind leads to crashes.

Partner coordination meetings cancelled

"Everyone's too busy this month." One cancellation becomes the norm. Wraparound coordination degrades into referrals that go nowhere.

Outcomes not measured or reported

"We know we're making a difference." Anecdotes replace evidence. Without measurement, hope is your only strategy.

Supervision sessions becoming "check-ins"

"Let's just do a quick update." Supervision without case review and skill coaching doesn't prevent drift. It enables it.

New staff "learning as they go"

"We'll train them on the job." Without structured pre-service training, every new generation of staff drifts further from the model.

17.7 The Cost of Fidelity

Quality assurance costs money. Training takes time. Supervision requires investment. Some leaders see fidelity as overhead. That thinking destroys programs.

The Cost of Drift

  • X Outcomes collapse -- The program stops working. Nobody notices until it's too late.
  • X Reputation destroyed -- Funders and partners lose trust when results don't match promises.
  • X Funding evaporates -- Can't call yourself evidence-based without fidelity to back it up.
  • X Navigators burn out -- Without proper support, good people leave.
  • X Families get hurt -- They trust you with their futures. Drift betrays that trust.

The Return on Fidelity

  • + Sustained outcomes -- The program keeps working as it grows.
  • + Costs drop at scale -- NFP proves it: $8,860 vs. $12,398 in original trials.
  • + Funders stay -- Demonstrated fidelity attracts continued investment.
  • + Staff stay -- Good supervision and support keeps navigators from burning out.
  • + Ready to replicate -- Documented fidelity makes expansion to new sites possible.

The Bottom Line

Drift kills programs. First effectiveness goes. Then funding. Then the program itself. Fidelity keeps outcomes strong, investment flowing, and impact growing.

Fidelity isn't overhead. It's the reason the program works. Period.

"The question isn't whether to scale evidence-based programs. It's how to scale them without losing their essence. The alternative -- scaling programs that have drifted into ineffectiveness -- wastes money and betrays the families they were designed to serve."
— Adapted from Horowitz et al., "Scaling Up Evidence-Based Practices"
Key Takeaway

Scaling with fidelity comes down to three commitments:

1 Define what can't bend -- The 8 Core Model Elements are the line.
2 Build the infrastructure -- Training. Supervision. Data. Coaching.
3 Watch for drift -- Catch warning signs before outcomes collapse.

NFP proves it's possible. Empower's framework gives you the blueprint.
