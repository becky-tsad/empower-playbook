Community Readiness Assessment

Evaluate whether your community has the foundations for successful Empower implementation

Instructions

  • 1. Complete this assessment with your core planning team (3-5 people recommended)
  • 2. Score each item honestly - this is a diagnostic tool, not a test
  • 3. Discuss any items where team members disagree significantly
  • 4. Use results to identify areas needing development before launch
  • 5. Reassess quarterly during planning phase

Assessment Information

Section 1: Political Will & Leadership

Assess the level of support and commitment from key decision-makers.

1.1 Do you have a champion in a position of authority who is committed to this initiative?

1.2 Is there alignment between community leadership and organizational leadership on the need for this program?

1.3 Is there political stability to sustain a 3-5 year initiative?

1.4 Do key leaders understand and accept the 18-24 month timeline for participant outcomes?

Section 2: Partner Ecosystem

Evaluate the strength of your potential partner network.

2.1 Do you have relationships with employers willing to participate?

2.2 Do you have access to childcare resources for participants?

2.3 Do you have access to transportation solutions?

2.4 Do you have workforce development/training partners identified?

2.5 Do you have relationships with benefits administrators (DHS, SNAP, Medicaid)?

Section 3: Funding & Resources

Assess your capacity to fund and resource the program.

3.1 Do you have seed funding for Year 1 operations ($150K-$250K)?

3.2 Is there a sustainability plan for Years 2-3?

3.3 Do you have access to emergency/barrier removal funds?

3.4 Do you have or can you acquire navigator capacity?

Section 4: Data & Technology Infrastructure

Assess your capacity to track outcomes and manage data. This is critical - poor data infrastructure kills programs.

4.1 Do you have a case management/data tracking system?

4.2 Can you track family-level (not just individual) outcomes?

4.3 Do you have capacity for benefits cliff modeling/tracking?

4.4 Do you have reporting capacity for funders and partners?

Section 5: Community Context

Understand your community's specific characteristics and needs.

5.1 What is your ALICE population estimate?

5.2 Do you understand your community's benefits cliff exposure?

5.3 Is there existing community trust and engagement with target population?

Score Interpretation

0-40 Points: Not Ready

Significant foundational work needed. Focus on building partnerships, securing leadership commitment, and addressing critical infrastructure gaps before proceeding.

41-60 Points: Emerging Readiness

Some foundations in place but gaps remain. Use this assessment to prioritize development areas. Consider a 6-12 month readiness phase before launch.

61-80 Points: Ready with Caveats

Strong foundations with some areas for improvement. Can proceed with implementation while actively addressing weaker areas. Monitor low-scoring items closely.

81-100 Points: Launch Ready

Excellent foundations in place. Ready to proceed with full implementation. Continue building on strengths while maintaining momentum.
