5-Minute Executive Summary

The Empower Playbook

The Problem

Rural working families are trapped between poverty wages and the benefits cliff. One raise can mean losing more in benefits than they gain in income. Traditional social services are siloed, urban-designed, and fail rural communities.

Over 3 million U.S. households face benefits cliffs—where a modest income increase triggers the loss of healthcare, childcare subsidies, housing assistance, or food benefits worth more than the raise itself. This creates a rational economic trap: families stay poor because climbing out costs too much.

In rural areas, these challenges compound: limited transportation options, fewer employers, thin social service networks, and the isolation that makes coordination nearly impossible with traditional approaches.

What Makes This Model Different

Most poverty programs measure activities (referrals made, workshops attended). The Empower Method measures outcomes: income increased, cliffs avoided, families achieving self-sufficiency.

This model evolved from 40+ years of Scott Miller's work (founder of Circles USA) across 100+ communities, refined through The Poverty Solution's technical assistance, and proven in the Upper Cumberland region of Tennessee—one of the most economically challenged rural areas in Appalachia.

The Four Non-Negotiables

Every successful implementation must include these four elements. They are the DNA of the model—without them, outcomes suffer.

  • 1.
    Benefits Cliff Navigation

    Map every family's specific cliff points. Plan raises strategically so income increases outpace benefit losses. Turn the cliff into a staircase.

  • 2.
    Navigator Model

    One dedicated person guides each family through the full 18-24 month journey. Not hand-offs between case managers—sustained relationship that builds trust and accountability.

  • 3.
    Wraparound Coordination

    Connect housing, childcare, transportation, healthcare, and employment in one coordinated plan. Address barriers together, not one at a time.

  • 4.
    Data Dashboard

    Real-time tracking of outcomes (not just activities). Shared across partners. Enables course correction and proves impact to funders.

Two-Generational Impact

Every family served includes children. When parents achieve economic stability, children see better educational outcomes, improved health, and a model for economic mobility. The 923 families served include 1,886 children—doubling the impact of every dollar invested.
