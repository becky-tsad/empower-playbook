Executive Summary

Traditional TANF programs focus on meeting federal work participation rates and moving families off assistance as quickly as possible. The result: families cycle between low-wage jobs and benefits, never achieving true economic stability.

The Empower Method transforms TANF from a compliance exercise into a pathway to sustainable self-sufficiency—meeting federal requirements while building family economic mobility.

Moving Beyond Compliance to Career Advancement

Federal work participation requirements can be met while also building long-term economic mobility. The key is reframing countable activities around career advancement:

Meeting WPR Through Quality Activities

Countable work activities that also build career mobility:

  • Subsidized employment in career-pathway sectors (healthcare, IT, skilled trades)
  • On-the-job training with employers committed to advancement
  • Vocational education in high-demand credential programs
  • Work experience that builds transferable skills and references
  • Job readiness combined with sector-specific skills training
  • Community service in organizations that hire participants
Strategic Insight

Use the 12-month vocational education allowance strategically—stack short-term credentials that each take 3-4 months. A CNA certificate in month 1-4, phlebotomy in months 5-8, and medical assistant in months 9-12 creates a healthcare career pathway within federal limits.

Two-Generation Strategies

Sustainable family mobility requires addressing parent and child needs simultaneously:

  • Aligned schedules: Parent work/training hours match children's school/childcare hours
  • Co-located services: Adult education at Head Start sites
  • Family financial coaching: Parents and teens learn together
  • Career exposure: Children see parents' workplaces and career growth
  • Educational expectations: Link parent advancement to child academic support

TANF Work Participation Rate Strategies

Meeting the federal 50% all-families and 90% two-parent WPR while building quality outcomes requires strategic caseload management:

WPR Calculation Optimization

Strategy Impact on WPR Implementation
Caseload Reduction Credit Reduces target rate Document all case closures due to earnings
Excess MOE Credit Further reduces target Maximize MOE spending documentation
Child-Only Cases Excluded from WPR Proper categorization at intake
SSI Pending Cases Can be excluded Track disability applications
Domestic Violence Waivers Temporary exclusion Implement FVO screening at intake

Participation Hour Strategies

Maximize countable hours while maintaining quality:

  • Core + Non-Core Balance: Use full 20 hours of non-core activities for job skills training and education
  • Paid Work Experience: Counts as core activity, provides income, builds employer relationships
  • Supervised Job Search: Up to 6 weeks per 12-month period (12 weeks for states exceeding WPR)
  • Vocational Education: Counts as core for 12 months lifetime—use strategically
  • Work Study: Countable at actual hours, keeps participants engaged in education
Critical: Time Limit Planning

Federal 60-month lifetime limit requires proactive planning. Begin career pathway development at intake—not month 48. State time limits may be shorter. Track each family's remaining months and accelerate advancement accordingly.

Engagement Over Sanctions

Sanction-heavy approaches damage outcomes and don't improve WPR long-term:

  • Replace sanction warnings with barrier assessment conversations
  • Implement "good cause" reviews before any sanction
  • Use conciliation periods to address underlying issues
  • Track sanction-to-engagement conversion rates
  • Measure long-term outcomes for sanctioned vs. supported families

Benefits Cliff Navigation

Benefits cliffs are the hidden force that keeps TANF families trapped. When a $1/hour raise costs a family $500/month in lost benefits, rational people don't take the raise—or they cycle back onto assistance.

Critical Cliff Points for TANF Families

Benefit Typical Cliff Point Impact of Loss
TANF Cash Varies by state (often 30-50% FPL) $200-500/month cash loss
Medicaid (Adult) 138% FPL (expansion states) $400-800/month healthcare value
SNAP 130% FPL (gross income) $300-600/month food benefit
Childcare Subsidy Often 200-250% FPL $800-1,500/month childcare cost
Housing Assistance Often 50-80% AMI $500-1,200/month rent increase
EITC Phase-Out $40-50K (varies by children) Up to $7,000/year tax credit loss

Cliff Navigation Process

  1. Map Current Benefits: Document every benefit the family receives with exact income thresholds
  2. Calculate Total Benefit Value: Many families don't realize their full benefit package value ($15-25K annually)
  3. Identify Cliff Points: Create visual map of where each benefit phases out
  4. Model Income Scenarios: Calculate net resources at different wage levels
  5. Find the "Clear Zone": Identify income level where work truly pays more than benefits
  6. Create Transition Plan: Sequence wage increases and benefit transitions strategically
The Clear Zone

For most TANF families, the "clear zone" where earned income truly exceeds benefit value is around $45,000-55,000 annually. Career pathways must aim for this target—not just minimum wage exit.

Transitional Benefits Coordination

Leverage every transitional benefit to smooth the cliff:

  • Transitional Medicaid: 12 months guaranteed coverage after TANF exit
  • Transitional Childcare: Many states offer 12-24 months post-TANF
  • SNAP Transitional Benefits: Earned income disregards, 12-month recertification
  • Earned Income Disregards: Maximize TANF income disregards to extend benefits
  • Individual Development Accounts: Build assets while on TANF without affecting eligibility

Navigator Integration with Case Management

The Career Mobility Coach role transforms traditional TANF case management from compliance monitoring to career advancement support:

Career Mobility Coach Model

Caseload Ratio: 1 coach per 25-30 families (maximum)

Duration: Same coach from intake through 12+ months post-exit

Key Responsibilities:

  • Conduct comprehensive family assessment beyond standard eligibility
  • Create career pathway plan aligned with family goals and labor market
  • Map benefits cliff points and develop navigation strategy
  • Coordinate barrier removal with community partners
  • Connect to employment and training in career-pathway sectors
  • Provide ongoing coaching (weekly minimum during active phase)
  • Monitor progress and adjust plans based on results
  • Continue support through transition off TANF and post-exit

Required Skills:

  • Understanding of TANF rules and work participation requirements
  • Knowledge of career pathways and local labor market
  • Benefits cliff navigation expertise
  • Trauma-informed engagement techniques
  • Motivational interviewing skills
  • Crisis intervention capabilities
  • Strong relationship-building with employers and partners

Integration with Existing Case Management

Function Eligibility Worker Career Mobility Coach
Eligibility Determination Primary responsibility Coordinate timing with career plan
WPR Documentation Verify and report Ensure quality activities are documented
Career Planning Refer to coach Primary responsibility
Barrier Removal Basic referrals Active coordination and follow-up
Employment Connection Compliance focus Career-pathway focus
Post-Exit Support Case closed Continues 12+ months
Critical Success Factor

Career Mobility Coaches must have protected time for family engagement. They cannot also carry eligibility determination responsibilities. Quality coaching requires focused relationship-building, not paperwork.

Partner Coordination Protocols

Each partner MOU should specify:

  • Designated contact: Named person for TANF referrals
  • Response time: 48-hour maximum for acknowledgment, 5 days to service
  • Priority access: TANF families get priority enrollment/service
  • Information sharing: Consent-based updates on service status
  • Coordination meetings: Monthly partner convenings
  • Outcome reporting: Quarterly data on referral completion rates

Employer Partner Development

Quality employment requires employer partnerships beyond job fairs:

  • Identify employers with career ladders and advancement opportunities
  • Negotiate on-the-job training agreements that count toward WPR
  • Create subsidized employment placements in career-pathway roles
  • Establish work experience sites that lead to hiring
  • Build relationships with sector-specific employer networks (healthcare, manufacturing, IT)
  • Develop employer commitment to benefits-friendly scheduling

Data Dashboard: TANF Outcomes and Mobility Metrics

Required Data Elements

Federal Compliance Metrics

  • Work participation rate (all families and two-parent)
  • Countable activity hours by type
  • Sanction rates and reasons
  • Case closure reasons (employment, time limit, sanction, other)
  • Time-on-assistance tracking

Mobility Metrics (Beyond Compliance)

  • Starting wage at employment vs. wage at 6/12/24 months
  • Career credential attainment
  • Income growth trajectory (% increase from baseline)
  • Benefits cliff events navigated
  • Total family resources (earnings + remaining benefits)
  • Return-to-TANF rate at 12 and 24 months post-exit

Family Stability Indicators

  • Housing stability (moves, eviction risk)
  • Childcare stability (arrangement changes, disruptions)
  • Child school attendance and performance
  • Healthcare coverage continuity
  • Food security status

MOE Considerations

  • Third-party MOE from community partners can count toward state requirement

  • Childcare and transportation provided by partners can count as MOE
  • Document all qualifying expenditures to maximize MOE credit and reduce WPR target

  • Families moving to sustainable wages above the benefits cliff
