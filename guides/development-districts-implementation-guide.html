Executive Summary

Development Districts are uniquely positioned to drive regional economic mobility. Spanning multiple counties, you coordinate economic development, workforce training, and community services across jurisdictional boundaries—exactly the scope needed for systemic poverty reduction.

The Empower Method transforms how Development Districts approach economic mobility—moving from fragmented services to coordinated, family-centered support that builds lasting self-sufficiency.

The Regional Advantage

Development Districts can coordinate across county lines, align workforce boards with navigator services, aggregate data regionally, and create economies of scale that individual counties cannot achieve alone.

The Four Non-Negotiables for Development Districts

1

Benefits Cliff Navigation at Regional Scale

Deploy cliff modeling tools across all counties. When participants hit income thresholds, navigators help them sequence wage increases with benefits transitions to prevent backward slides.

2

Regional Navigator Network

Navigators embedded in each county, supervised regionally. Low caseloads (1:30-40 families), long-term relationships (2+ years), and shared expertise across the service area.

3

Workforce Board Alignment

Integrate navigator services with WIOA programs, sector partnerships, and employer engagement. Career pathways + family stability = sustainable employment.

4

Regional Data Dashboard

Aggregate economic mobility data across counties. Track participants from intake through self-sufficiency. Benchmark outcomes across communities to identify and spread best practices.

Multi-County Coordination Strategies

Effective regional implementation requires intentional coordination across county boundaries. Development Districts must build structures that enable resource sharing while respecting local contexts.

Regional Governance Structure

Level Composition Function
Regional Steering Committee County officials, workforce board, major employers, community anchors Strategic direction, policy alignment, funding coordination
Operations Team District staff, navigator supervisors, partner liaisons Day-to-day coordination, issue resolution, quality assurance
County Implementation Teams Local navigators, county partners, employers Direct service delivery, local relationship management

Resource Sharing Across Counties

  • Shared Training: Regional navigator training events, peer learning circles, quarterly skill-building
  • Pooled Expertise: Specialists (housing, benefits, workforce) available to navigators across all counties
  • Common Data Platform: Single dashboard aggregating outcomes across the service area
  • Regional Employer Partnerships: Coordinate with employers operating across multiple counties
  • Transportation Solutions: Regional ride-share programs, coordinated transit connections
Coordination Principle

Families don't experience poverty by county—opportunities for jobs, childcare, and healthcare often cross jurisdictional lines. Regional coordination ensures families can access the best resources regardless of where they're located.

Managing County Variations

Each county has different resources, political contexts, and community dynamics. Successful regional implementation:

  • Adapts intake and outreach strategies to local culture
  • Leverages existing trusted relationships in each community
  • Respects county-level decision-making while maintaining regional standards
  • Celebrates local successes while sharing learnings across boundaries
  • Maintains consistent data collection for regional comparison

Regional Workforce Development Integration

Development Districts often house or closely coordinate with Workforce Development Boards. This creates a powerful opportunity to align navigator services with WIOA programs, sector partnerships, and employer engagement.

Integration Model

Workforce Component Navigator Integration Outcome
WIOA Adult & Dislocated Worker Navigators co-enroll participants, provide wraparound stability support Higher training completion and job retention
Career Centers Navigators stationed in or closely linked to one-stop centers Seamless referrals, family needs addressed on-site
Sector Partnerships Navigators understand industry pathways, connect families to opportunities Pipeline into high-demand occupations
Youth Programs Navigator support for families of youth participants Family stability improves youth outcomes
Employer Services Navigators help employees navigate benefits cliffs during wage increases Reduced turnover, promotion acceptance

Benefits Cliff Navigation with Employers

Partner with employers to address the benefits cliff that prevents workers from accepting raises and promotions:

  • Train HR staff to recognize when employees may face cliff situations
  • Create referral pathways from employers to navigators
  • Model wage increase timing to minimize benefits loss
  • Explore employer-sponsored benefits to bridge gaps (childcare subsidies, transportation)
  • Document ROI: reduced turnover, increased promotion acceptance
Employer Value Proposition

Employers lose 40% of entry-level workers within 90 days. Navigator support during the critical first year of employment reduces turnover, increases productivity, and builds a more stable workforce.

Career Pathway Alignment

Navigators help participants see and pursue career pathways, not just jobs:

  • Map regional career pathways in high-demand sectors
  • Connect short-term credentials to long-term career advancement
  • Coordinate benefits cliff navigation with each career step
  • Track participant progress along pathways over multiple years

Navigator Network Across Service Area

Regional Staffing Model

Navigator Ratio: 1 navigator per 30-40 active families

Relationship Duration: Same navigator for 2+ years minimum

Role Scope Key Responsibilities
Regional Program Director Entire service area Strategy, funding, regional partnerships, data analysis
Navigator Supervisor 3-4 counties (6-10 navigators) Case consultation, quality assurance, training, partner coordination
County Navigator Single county or sub-region Direct family support, local partner relationships, data entry
Regional Specialists Entire service area Deep expertise in housing, benefits, workforce, shared across counties

Navigator Placement Strategy

  • Embedded at Access Points: Workforce centers, community health centers, schools, social service offices
  • Mobile Capacity: Regular office hours in rural communities without permanent placement
  • Virtual Options: Phone and video support for remote areas with connectivity
  • Home Visits: Regular in-home meetings to understand family context

Rural Coverage Solutions

Rural counties present unique challenges. Strategies include:

  • Navigator "circuit riding" with scheduled days in each community
  • Partnership with trusted local institutions (churches, libraries, schools)
  • Group sessions for common needs (benefits applications, job search)
  • Coordination with existing home-visiting programs
  • Technology-enabled support where broadband exists
Hiring Principle

Hire navigators from the communities they'll serve. Local knowledge, existing relationships, and cultural understanding accelerate trust-building and improve outcomes.

Economic Development Alignment

Development Districts coordinate economic development strategy across their service area. Aligning Empower implementation with economic development priorities creates mutual reinforcement.

Strategic Alignment Points

Economic Development Priority Empower Contribution
Business Recruitment Demonstrate workforce stability and support infrastructure to prospective employers
Existing Business Expansion Navigate benefits cliffs to enable worker advancement; reduce turnover costs
Entrepreneurship Support aspiring entrepreneurs through income volatility during business launch
Talent Retention Help workers and families build roots through stability, reducing out-migration
Regional Competitiveness Economic mobility data demonstrates community investment in human capital

Business and Employer Engagement

  • Employer Advisory Council: Quarterly meetings to align navigator services with employer needs
  • HR Partnership Program: Direct referral pathways from employers to navigators
  • Retention Support: Navigator check-ins during first 90 days of employment
  • Advancement Navigation: Benefits cliff modeling before wage increases
  • Childcare Solutions: Coordinate with employers on childcare access and subsidies

Rural Economic Opportunity

Connect rural residents to regional economic opportunities:

  • Transportation solutions for jobs in regional centers
  • Remote work support for employers with flexible policies
  • Micro-enterprise and self-employment navigation
  • Agricultural worker support through seasonal income fluctuations
  • Healthcare and childcare access that enables workforce participation
Common Pitfall: Don't treat economic development and poverty reduction as separate programs. Workforce stability IS economic development. Employer partnerships serve both goals.

Community Partner Integration Across Counties

Development Districts must build partner networks that span the entire service area while respecting local relationships. This requires both regional coordination and county-level connection.

Partnership Agreement Framework

Establish MOUs with key partners that include:

  • Designated point of contact for navigator referrals
  • Response time expectations (48 hours for non-emergency)
  • Information sharing protocols (with participant consent)
  • Participation in regional coordination meetings
  • Outcome data sharing for dashboard integration
  • Cross-training opportunities

Rural Transportation Solutions

Transportation is the #1 barrier in rural regions. Strategies include:

  • Regional demand-response transit coordination
  • Volunteer driver programs with mileage reimbursement
  • Vehicle repair and car purchase assistance programs
  • Employer van pools and ride-sharing facilitation
  • Telehealth and virtual service delivery where appropriate
  • Co-located services to reduce trip needs

Data Dashboard for Regional Economic Mobility Metrics

A regional data dashboard enables Development Districts to track outcomes, identify patterns, allocate resources, and demonstrate impact to funders and policymakers.

Participant-Level Data

  • Household composition and demographics
  • Income and employment status (tracked over time)
  • Benefits received (SNAP, Medicaid, housing, childcare)
  • Benefits cliff proximity and transitions
  • Education and credential attainment
  • Housing stability status
  • Transportation access
  • Childcare arrangements

Service Delivery Metrics

  • Families enrolled by county
  • Navigator caseload and contact frequency
  • Referrals made and completed by category
  • Benefits cliff navigations completed
  • Training enrollments and completions
  • Employment placements and retention

Connecting Rural Communities to Opportunities

Development Districts serve as the critical bridge between rural communities and regional economic opportunity. The Empower Model, implemented at regional scale, ensures that:

  • Families in every county have access to navigator support, regardless of local capacity
  • Regional employment opportunities are accessible through coordinated transportation
  • Career pathways extend from local entry points to regional advancement
  • Benefits cliff navigation enables workers to accept better jobs anywhere in the region
  • Data-driven decision-making optimizes resource allocation across communities
  • Success stories in one county inform strategies across the service area
