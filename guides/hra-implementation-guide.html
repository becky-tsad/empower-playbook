The HRA Opportunity

Energy assistance applicants are already self-identifying as economically vulnerable. Every LIHEAP intake is a chance to assess the whole family, map benefits cliffs, and create a pathway to stability. HRAs that seize this moment see families achieve lasting self-sufficiency rather than returning year after year.

The Four Non-Negotiables for HRAs

1

Benefits Cliff Navigation

Map every family's cliff exposure across all programs. Use cliff analysis tools to plan income increases that don't trigger devastating benefit losses. This is the difference between cycling families through programs forever and achieving true economic mobility.

2

Navigator Model

Assign dedicated Navigators to families with 1:40-50 caseload ratios. Same Navigator for the entire journey—from crisis through stability to self-sufficiency. Relationship continuity is essential.

3

Wraparound Coordination

Every service—energy, housing, food, healthcare, childcare—exists to clear the path to a livable wage job. Families experience one integrated support system focused on employment outcomes.

4

Data Dashboard

Track family progress across all dimensions—not just program outputs. Measure income growth, cliff navigation success, housing stability, and time to self-sufficiency.

Integration Principle

Every program touchpoint should trigger a question: "What else does this family need?" The family's first contact with any HRA program should open the door to all relevant services.

Benefits Cliff Navigation Protocols

Benefits cliffs are the hidden trap that keeps families cycling through HRA programs year after year. When families earn more income, they often lose benefits worth more than their raise—creating a rational disincentive to advancement.

Critical Cliff Points

Program Cliff Threshold Benefit Value at Risk
SNAP (Food Stamps) 130% FPL gross income $3,000-7,000/year
Medicaid (Adults) 138% FPL $6,000-15,000/year
Childcare Subsidy Varies (often 200-250% FPL) $8,000-20,000/year
Housing Assistance 50-80% AMI $5,000-15,000/year
LIHEAP 150% FPL (typically) $500-2,000/year
EITC Phase-Out Gradual reduction zone Up to $7,430/year

Navigator Cliff Navigation Process

  1. Map Current Benefits: Document all benefits the family currently receives with income thresholds
  2. Calculate Total Cliff Exposure: Sum the value of all benefits at risk at each income level
  3. Identify Safe Income Targets: Find income levels where net resources increase (wages gained exceed benefits lost)
  4. Sequence Advancement: Plan income increases strategically—sometimes smaller raises that preserve benefits, sometimes larger jumps that clear multiple cliffs
  5. Build Replacement Resources: Before cliff, help family build savings, access employer benefits, or find cliff mitigation programs
  6. Monitor Transitions: Track family through income changes, adjust plan as needed
Common Cliff Trap

A family at 125% FPL receiving SNAP, Medicaid, and childcare subsidy could face $25,000+ in annual benefit losses from a modest raise. Without cliff navigation, this family rationally rejects advancement—and remains HRA clients indefinitely.

Navigator Staffing Model

Family Navigator

Caseload: 40-50 families per Navigator

Commitment: Same Navigator for entire journey (2-4 years typical)

Core Responsibilities:

  • Conduct comprehensive family assessments at intake
  • Create family stability plans with benefits cliff analysis
  • Navigate benefits enrollment and renewal across all programs
  • Coordinate with employment, housing, and other partners
  • Provide ongoing coaching and crisis intervention
  • Track progress and outcomes in data system
  • Advocate for families with landlords, employers, and agencies
  • Facilitate warm handoffs to partner services

Required Qualifications:

  • Bachelor's degree in social work, human services, or related field (or equivalent experience)
  • 2+ years experience working with low-income families
  • Deep knowledge of public benefits systems
  • Strong relationship-building and communication skills
  • Cultural competency and trauma-informed approach
  • Proficiency with data systems and case documentation
  • Valid driver's license for home visits

Training Requirements:

  • Empower Navigator Certification (40 hours)
  • Benefits Cliff Analysis Tool Training (8 hours)
  • Motivational Interviewing (16 hours)
  • Trauma-Informed Care (8 hours)
  • Local Resource Network Orientation (8 hours)
  • Financial Coaching Fundamentals (8 hours)

Navigator Supervisor

Ratio: 1 supervisor per 5-6 Navigators

Key Responsibilities:

  • Provide clinical supervision and case consultation
  • Review family stability plans and cliff analyses
  • Manage partner relationships and escalations
  • Monitor caseload data and outcomes
  • Facilitate weekly team meetings
  • Ensure fidelity to Empower methodology
Caseload Reality

40-50 families is the maximum for effective navigation. Higher caseloads force transactional service delivery—exactly what we're trying to avoid. Protect Navigator caseloads to preserve relationship-based outcomes.

Partner Coordination Structure

  • Weekly Case Conferences: Navigators bring complex cases for multi-partner problem-solving
  • Monthly Partner Meetings: All partners review referral flows, identify gaps, improve coordination
  • Shared Data Dashboard: Track referrals made and completed, time to service, outcomes
  • Warm Handoffs: Navigator introduces family to partner, ensures connection before stepping back
  • Co-Location: Where possible, partner staff on-site at HRA for seamless access

Memorandum of Understanding Template

Each partner MOU should include:

  • Designated point of contact for referrals
  • Referral response time commitment (within 48-72 hours)
  • Information sharing protocols with family consent
  • Participation in monthly coordination meetings
  • Outcome data sharing requirements
  • Annual review and renewal process
