Executive Summary

Public Housing Authorities serve some of the nation's most economically vulnerable families. Yet traditional PHA operations often inadvertently trap residents in poverty through rent calculation cliffs, disconnected services, and program rules that penalize income growth.

The Empower Method transforms PHAs from housing administrators into economic mobility engines—helping residents build income, assets, and pathways to housing stability and self-sufficiency.

The Core Insight

The 30% income rule creates one of the steepest benefits cliffs in the safety net. For every dollar a resident earns, they lose 30 cents in increased rent—plus potential losses in SNAP, Medicaid, and childcare subsidies. Without cliff navigation, income growth triggers housing instability.

The Four Non-Negotiables for PHAs

1

Benefits Cliff Navigation for Rent Calculations

Help residents understand and plan for rent increases before they happen. Use cliff modeling tools to show the true cost of income changes and identify pathways that maximize net benefit.

2

Resident Navigator Model

Dedicated navigators (1 per 75-100 households) who build long-term relationships, coordinate services, and walk alongside families through economic transitions. Not case managers—partners.

3

FSS Program Transformation

Redesign FSS from a compliance program into a genuine wealth-building opportunity. Proactive escrow building, goal-aligned services, and navigation support from enrollment to completion.

4

Data-Driven Mobility Tracking

Dashboard tracking income growth, escrow accumulation, employment changes, educational advancement, and housing outcomes. Measure what matters: economic mobility, not just occupancy.

Integration with HCV and Public Housing Programs

Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) Program

HCV participants face unique mobility challenges: income reporting requirements, landlord relationships, and portability decisions. The Empower approach enhances HCV administration:

Traditional Approach Empower Approach
Annual recertification focuses on compliance Recertification becomes economic coaching opportunity
Income increases trigger immediate rent burden Cliff modeling prepares families for transitions
FSS offered but under-enrolled FSS actively promoted with navigator support
Portability seen as administrative burden Portability used strategically for opportunity moves
Limited connection to employment services Integrated workforce and education referrals

Public Housing Program

Public housing residents often have deeper service needs and longer tenure. The Empower model adapts for on-site implementation:

  • On-Site Navigator Presence: Navigators maintain regular office hours at properties, building trust through consistent availability
  • Community Building: Leverage property-based community for peer support, resident leadership, and collective economic advancement
  • Coordinated Services: Partner with on-site service providers (Head Start, senior services, health clinics) for warm handoffs
  • Rent Structure Education: Help residents understand flat rent vs. income-based rent options and when each benefits them
MTW Opportunity

Moving to Work PHAs have significant flexibility to redesign rent structures, FSS incentives, and self-sufficiency programs. MTW activities like stepped rent, income disregards, and alternative recertification schedules can dramatically reduce cliff effects.

Program-Specific Strategies

For HCV Programs:

  • Use briefing sessions to introduce economic mobility services
  • Include cliff navigation in portability counseling
  • Partner with landlords on resident retention during income growth
  • Leverage interim recertifications as coaching touchpoints

For Public Housing:

  • Integrate navigator services with property management
  • Coordinate with resident councils on mobility initiatives
  • Use community rooms for financial coaching workshops
  • Connect with ROSS-SC grants for service coordination funding

FSS Program Transformation

The Family Self-Sufficiency program is the federal government's primary vehicle for resident economic mobility—but most PHAs see low enrollment, high dropout rates, and minimal escrow payouts. The Empower approach transforms FSS outcomes:

Current FSS Challenges

  • Average national graduation rate: under 35%
  • Many participants never build meaningful escrow
  • Limited coordinator capacity (1 per 50+ participants)
  • Goals often generic, not aligned with resident aspirations
  • Disconnected from broader cliff navigation

The Empower FSS Model

1

Proactive Recruitment with Clear Value Proposition

Show residents exactly how FSS escrow works using their current income. Model potential escrow accumulation over 5 years. Make the financial benefit tangible and compelling.

2

Goal-Aligned Individualized Training Plans

Work with residents to identify their actual goals—not just "get a job" but specific careers, wage targets, and timelines. Connect training and education to those goals.

3

Integrated Cliff Navigation

Map how income increases affect rent, SNAP, Medicaid, and childcare together. Identify the income ranges that maximize total household resources. Plan career steps accordingly.

4

Escrow as Wealth-Building Tool

Frame escrow not as program compliance but as forced savings. Connect to financial coaching. Help residents plan escrow use: homeownership, education, business startup, emergency fund.

Resident Navigator Model

Navigator Role Definition

Ratio: 1 Navigator per 75-100 households (intensive) or 1 per 150-200 (standard)

Commitment: Same navigator works with families for duration of program participation

Core Navigator Responsibilities:

  • Conduct comprehensive economic assessments at intake
  • Create personalized cliff navigation and mobility plans
  • Meet regularly with households (monthly minimum, more during transitions)
  • Coordinate referrals to employment, education, and support services
  • Track progress and adjust plans based on changing circumstances
  • Advocate for residents with landlords, employers, and service providers
  • Prepare residents for rent increases and benefits transitions
  • Support FSS participants through to graduation
  • Document outcomes in mobility tracking system

Required Qualifications:

  • Experience working with low-income populations
  • Understanding of affordable housing programs and benefits systems
  • Strong relationship-building and motivational interviewing skills
  • Cultural competency and trauma-informed approach
  • Data management and documentation capabilities
  • Bachelor's degree preferred; relevant experience may substitute

Training Requirements:

  • Empower Navigator Certification (40 hours)
  • Benefits Cliff Modeling Tool Training (8 hours)
  • HUD Housing Program Fundamentals (16 hours)
  • Motivational Interviewing (16 hours)
  • Trauma-Informed Care (8 hours)
  • Local Resource Network Orientation (8 hours)
Common Pitfall

Don't confuse navigators with case managers. Case managers ensure compliance; navigators build relationships and walk alongside residents through economic transitions. The relationship is the intervention.

Navigator Workflow

Activity Frequency Purpose
Initial Assessment At enrollment Understand full economic picture, goals, barriers
Mobility Plan Development Within 30 days Create individualized roadmap with milestones
Regular Check-ins Monthly minimum Track progress, address barriers, adjust plan
Cliff Modeling Sessions Before income changes Prepare for rent and benefit impacts
Service Coordination Ongoing Warm referrals, follow-up on connections
Transition Support During major changes Intensive support during job changes, moves

Benefits Cliff Navigation

The Housing Cliff: Understanding the 30% Rule

The 30% income rule for rent calculation creates a predictable but often devastating cliff. For every additional dollar earned, residents pay 30 cents more in rent. Combined with other benefit phase-outs, effective marginal tax rates can exceed 80%.

Sample Cliff Analysis: Single Parent, Two Children, HCV

Annual Income Rent Portion SNAP Childcare Subsidy Net Available
$15,000 $375/mo $535/mo Full $20,420
$20,000 $500/mo $410/mo Full $22,020
$25,000 $625/mo $285/mo Reduced $22,920
$30,000 $750/mo $160/mo Lost $21,320
$35,000 $875/mo $0 Lost $24,500

Note: This family is actually worse off at $30,000 than at $25,000 due to childcare cliff. Values illustrative; actual amounts vary by location.

Cliff Navigation Strategies

Before Income Increases:

  • Model the full impact across all benefits (housing, SNAP, Medicaid, childcare)
  • Identify "cliff zones" where income gains are effectively erased
  • Plan career steps to skip over or quickly pass through cliff zones
  • Time income changes strategically (e.g., after childcare costs decrease)

PHA Policy Tools:

  • Earned Income Disregards: Exclude portion of new income from rent calculation
  • Stepped Rent: Gradual rent increases over time rather than immediate
  • Ceiling Rents: Cap rent at market rate regardless of income
  • FSS Escrow: Rent increases build savings instead of just cost
  • Interim Recertification Timing: Strategic scheduling of income reporting
The MTW Advantage

MTW agencies can implement alternative rent structures that dramatically reduce cliff effects. Tiered rent, minimum rent floors, and simplified income calculations all help residents plan income growth without penalty.

Partner Coordination for Cliff Navigation:

  • Coordinate with SNAP office on transitional benefits
  • Connect to Medicaid expansion or marketplace navigation
  • Identify childcare assistance phase-out thresholds
  • Access EITC and CTC through tax preparation partnerships

  • Study Support: Tutoring connections, study space access, peer study groups

Homeownership Pathways and Positive Move-Outs

Homeownership Preparation

For residents with homeownership goals, coordinate a comprehensive pathway:

Phase 1: Foundation Building (12-24 months before purchase)

  • Credit assessment and repair planning
  • Savings plan development (down payment, closing costs, reserves)
  • Budget analysis for sustainable homeownership costs
  • HUD-approved homeownership counseling enrollment
  • FSS escrow accumulation strategy

Phase 2: Active Preparation (6-12 months before purchase)

  • Mortgage pre-qualification
  • Down payment assistance program navigation
  • Home search in opportunity neighborhoods
  • Home inspection and maintenance education
  • HCV Homeownership Program enrollment (if applicable)

Phase 3: Purchase and Transition

  • Closing process support
  • Utility setup and move coordination
  • Home maintenance planning
  • Post-purchase counseling connection
  • Celebration of achievement
HCV Homeownership Option

The HCV Homeownership Program allows voucher holders to use their subsidy toward mortgage payments instead of rent. This can make homeownership accessible at lower income levels while maintaining housing stability during the transition.

Graduated Rent Phase-Out Strategies

Help residents transition successfully off assistance:

  • Stepped Increases: Gradual rent increases over 12-24 months rather than immediate market rate
  • Transition Vouchers: Short-term assistance during program exit
  • Market-Ready Planning: Budget analysis for full market rent sustainability
  • Savings Accumulation: Build reserves before assistance ends
  • Location Strategy: Identify affordable market-rate options in opportunity areas

Positive Move-Out Outcomes

Define and track positive exits from assisted housing:

Partner Agreement Elements

Formalize relationships through MOUs that include:

  • Designated point of contact for referrals
  • Response time expectations (48-72 hours for initial contact)
  • Information sharing protocols (with resident consent)
  • Regular coordination meeting participation (monthly or quarterly)
  • Outcome reporting and feedback loops
  • Warm handoff procedures for high-priority referrals

Coordination Structure

  • Monthly Partner Meetings: Review referral trends, troubleshoot barriers, share updates
  • Case Conferences: As-needed meetings for complex household situations
  • Annual Partner Summit: Evaluate outcomes, refine partnerships, plan improvements
  • Shared Dashboard Access: Real-time visibility into referral status and outcomes

ROI Calculation

Each household that successfully transitions to market-rate housing or homeownership frees a voucher or unit for the waiting list—often valued at $8,000-15,000 annually. A 10% increase in positive move-outs generates significant program capacity and demonstrates community impact.
