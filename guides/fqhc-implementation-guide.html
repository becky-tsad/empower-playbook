Executive Summary

FQHCs and Community Health Centers already serve patients facing the greatest health disparities. But clinical care alone cannot address the root causes: unstable housing, food insecurity, unemployment, and the benefits cliffs that keep families trapped in poverty.

The Empower Method transforms SDOH screening from a checkbox into a pathway to economic mobility—with Community Health Workers serving as economic mobility navigators.

SDOH Screening as Gateway to Economic Mobility

Most health centers already conduct SDOH screening. The Empower Method transforms screening from data collection into an economic mobility assessment—the first step in a sustained support relationship.

Recommended Screening Protocol

Domain Key Questions Navigator Action Trigger
Housing Housing stability, utility shutoffs, living conditions Any instability indicator
Food Security Food access, SNAP enrollment, nutrition quality Food insecurity past 12 months
Transportation Reliable transportation, missed appointments Transportation barrier to care
Employment/Income Employment status, income stability, benefits cliff proximity Unemployment or benefits cliff risk
Healthcare Coverage Insurance type, coverage gaps, cost barriers Uninsured or Medicaid cliff risk
Childcare Childcare access, cost burden, reliability Childcare barrier to employment/health
Safety Domestic violence, neighborhood safety Any safety concern

Beyond Screening: The Navigator Handoff

When SDOH screening identifies needs, the traditional response is a resource list. The Empower Method response:

  1. Immediate Warm Introduction — Patient meets their assigned CHW Navigator before leaving the appointment
  2. Comprehensive Assessment — Navigator completes full economic mobility assessment including benefits cliff analysis
  3. Economic Mobility Plan — Co-created plan addressing immediate needs AND long-term stability goals
  4. Sustained Relationship — Regular check-ins, not just crisis response
The Difference

Traditional: "You screened positive for food insecurity. Here's a list of food banks."
Empower Method: "You screened positive for food insecurity. Let me introduce you to Maria, your navigator. She'll help you get SNAP benefits enrolled today, and work with you over the next 6 months to address the income issues causing the food insecurity."

Community Health Worker Navigator Model

The CHW Navigator role expands traditional CHW functions to include sustained economic mobility support. This is not case management—it's relationship-based navigation toward family stability.

CHW Navigator Role

Ratio: 1 Navigator per 40 active patients

Commitment: Same patients for minimum 12 months; continuity is critical

Core Responsibilities:

  • Receive warm handoffs from clinical teams for SDOH-positive patients
  • Conduct comprehensive economic mobility assessments
  • Develop co-created economic mobility plans with patients
  • Navigate benefits cliff transitions (Medicaid to Marketplace, SNAP to employment)
  • Make warm referrals to community partners and follow through to completion
  • Conduct regular check-ins (weekly to monthly based on need)
  • Document interventions and outcomes in EHR
  • Participate in care team huddles and case conferences

Required Competencies:

  • CHW certification (state requirements vary)
  • Deep understanding of public benefits systems (SNAP, Medicaid, TANF, housing)
  • Benefits cliff navigation and planning skills
  • Motivational interviewing and trauma-informed approaches
  • Cultural and linguistic competency for patient population
  • EHR documentation proficiency
  • Community resource knowledge and relationship-building

Training Requirements:

  • Empower Navigator Certification (40 hours)
  • Benefits Cliff Tool Training (8 hours)
  • Trauma-Informed Care (8 hours)
  • Motivational Interviewing (16 hours)
  • Local Resource Network Orientation (8 hours)
  • EHR/Documentation Training (4 hours)
Critical Success Factor: CHW Navigators must have protected time for patient navigation. If they are pulled into front desk coverage, interpreter duties, or other operational needs, the navigator function fails. Protect the caseload ratio and navigator time.

Healthcare Coverage Cliff Navigation

The Medicaid cliff is one of the most dangerous benefits cliffs. When patients' income rises above Medicaid thresholds, they can lose coverage entirely—often while managing chronic conditions that require continuous care.

The Medicaid-to-Marketplace Transition

Scenario Risk Navigator Response
Patient gets job/raise pushing income above Medicaid limit Coverage loss mid-chronic disease treatment Pre-plan transition; identify marketplace options; enroll before Medicaid ends
Medicaid redetermination (annual or unwinding) Administrative churn causing coverage gaps Proactive redetermination support; document submission; appeal assistance
Patient entering "donut hole" (income too high for Medicaid, too low for subsidized marketplace) Unaffordable coverage options Benefits cliff mapping; income optimization; explore all coverage options
Loss of Medicaid for children in family Pediatric care disruption CHIP enrollment; family marketplace plan analysis

Navigator Protocol for Coverage Transitions

  1. Early Warning — Identify patients approaching Medicaid income limits through employment/income discussions
  2. Benefits Cliff Mapping — Model the full impact of income change on all benefits (healthcare, SNAP, childcare, housing)
  3. Coverage Option Analysis — Research marketplace plans, employer coverage, CHIP options
  4. Transition Planning — Time the transition to minimize coverage gaps; coordinate with providers
  5. Enrollment Support — Assist with marketplace enrollment, premium tax credit applications
  6. Continuity Assurance — Ensure prescriptions, specialist referrals, and treatment plans transfer
Billing Opportunity

Many state Medicaid programs now reimburse for CHW services, including care coordination and benefits navigation. Document all navigator activities to maximize reimbursement. Some states allow billing for "bridging" services during coverage transitions.

Warm Referral Protocols

A referral is not a handoff—it's an introduction. Cold referrals (giving patients a phone number) have completion rates under 30%. Warm referrals with navigator follow-through exceed 70% completion.

Warm Referral Process

Step Action Timeline
1. Identify Need Navigator identifies specific resource need from assessment During assessment
2. Contact Partner Navigator calls partner organization, confirms availability, explains patient situation (with consent) Same day as assessment
3. Three-Way Introduction Navigator introduces patient to partner contact (phone or in-person) Within 48 hours
4. Appointment Scheduling Navigator helps schedule appointment and addresses barriers (transportation, childcare) At introduction
5. Confirmation Navigator confirms appointment with patient day before Day before appointment
6. Follow-Up Navigator checks in after appointment to confirm service received Within 3 days of appointment
7. Loop Closure Navigator documents outcome and determines if need was met Within 1 week

Partner Expectations

Community partners must agree to:

  • Designate a specific point of contact for warm referrals
  • Respond to navigator calls within 24 hours
  • Accept three-way introductions and prioritize referred patients
  • Report back to navigator on service delivery (with patient consent)
  • Participate in monthly partner coordination meetings
When Referrals Fail: If a patient cannot access a referred service (waitlist, eligibility, transportation), the navigator does not close the referral. Alternative resources are identified, or the navigator advocates for access. The need—not the referral—is the unit of measurement.

Community Partner Integration

Effective SDOH navigation requires deep relationships with community organizations across all domains. Build formal partnerships with MOUs that define referral protocols, response times, and outcome reporting.

Employment Workforce Development, WIOA Programs, Vocational Rehab, Employers Job training, placement, career coaching, benefits cliff counseling

Monthly Partner Coordination Meeting

Convene all partners monthly to:

  • Review complex cases requiring multi-agency coordination
  • Identify systemic barriers and advocate for policy changes
  • Share resource updates (new programs, waitlist status, funding changes)
  • Strengthen relationships and referral pathways
  • Celebrate successes and problem-solve challenges
Employment as a Health Intervention

Stable employment improves health outcomes across nearly every measure. Partner with workforce development to treat unemployment as a health condition requiring intervention—with the same urgency as uncontrolled diabetes.

Chronic Disease Management Through Stability

Uncontrolled chronic disease is often uncontrolled social circumstances. Address the root causes to improve clinical outcomes.

The Stability-Health Connection

Social Factor Health Impact Navigator Intervention
Food Insecurity 2x higher diabetes rates; poor glycemic control SNAP enrollment; medically tailored meals; food pharmacy programs
Housing Instability 3x higher chronic disease burden; medication storage issues Housing stabilization; utility assistance; healthy housing advocacy
Transportation Barriers Missed appointments; medication gaps; delayed care NEMT enrollment; bus passes; appointment scheduling optimization
Unemployment/Poverty Chronic stress; inability to afford healthy choices Employment connection; income supports; stress reduction
Coverage Gaps Medication non-adherence; avoided care; disease progression Coverage navigation; prescription assistance; sliding fee access

Mental Health and Substance Use Integration

Behavioral health conditions both cause and result from social instability. The navigator model must integrate:

  • Depression/Anxiety Screening — PHQ-9 and GAD-7 as standard alongside SDOH screening
  • Substance Use Screening — SBIRT integration with warm handoffs to treatment
  • Trauma-Informed Approach — All navigator interactions recognize trauma prevalence
  • Co-located Services — Behavioral health available at same site when possible
  • Peer Support Integration — Peer recovery specialists for SUD populations
The Stability Prescription

For patients with uncontrolled chronic disease, prescribe stability alongside medications. A stable home, reliable food, and continuous coverage are as important as metformin for diabetes control.

ROI for Health Centers

Reducing one preventable ED visit saves $1,500-$3,000. Preventing one hospitalization saves $10,000+. Navigator programs typically achieve ROI of 3:1 or higher through reduced acute utilization and improved chronic disease outcomes.
