Executive Summary

Family economic instability is the #1 predictor of dropout risk—more than academic performance. When parents face eviction, lose childcare for siblings, or work multiple jobs, students miss school, can't focus, and eventually disengage.

The Empower Method addresses student success by wrapping support around the entire family—not just the student.

The Core Insight

Addressing family economic barriers IS addressing student success. Students whose families receive navigator support are 2.5x more likely to enroll in post-secondary education.

The Four Non-Negotiables in Education

1

Benefits Cliff Navigation → Family Stability Planning

When families face benefits cliffs, students face attendance cliffs. Help parents navigate income changes so children don't become collateral damage.

2

Navigator Model → Family Success Coach

One Family Success Coach per 30-40 at-risk families. Same person for 4+ years—elementary through middle school transition, or middle through high school.

3

Wraparound Coordination → Community Schools Model

Coordinate housing, food security, healthcare, mental health, after-school care, transportation, parent employment. School becomes the hub.

4

Data Dashboard → Early Warning System

Track attendance patterns, grade trends, behavior incidents, family stability indicators. Predict dropout risk 2-3 years before it happens.

Implementation Timeline

1
Foundation Phase
Months 1-3
  • Secure leadership buy-in from superintendent and principals
  • Identify pilot schools (start with 1-2 high-need schools)
  • Hire Family Success Coaches (1 per 30-40 families)
  • Establish community partner agreements
  • Set up early warning data system
  • Train coaches on Empower methodology and benefits cliff navigation
2
Pilot Launch
Months 4-6
  • Identify at-risk students using early warning indicators
  • Conduct family intake assessments
  • Create family stability plans with benefits cliff maps
  • Begin regular home visits and school check-ins
  • Connect families to community resources
  • Establish weekly coordination meetings with teachers
3
Scale & Refine
Months 7-12
  • Track attendance, grades, and family stability outcomes
  • Refine intervention protocols based on data
  • Document success stories for expansion case
  • Plan expansion to additional schools
  • Develop sustainability funding strategy
  • Create peer learning network across schools
4
District-Wide Implementation
Year 2+
  • Expand to all high-need schools
  • Integrate with district strategic plan
  • Build college/career pipeline connections
  • Pursue state and federal funding streams
  • Measure long-term graduation and enrollment outcomes

Early Warning System: Key Indicators

Effective dropout prevention requires identifying at-risk students 2-3 years before crisis. Track these indicators to identify families needing support:

Attendance Indicators

Indicator Yellow Flag Red Flag
Chronic Absenteeism Missing 10-15% of school days Missing 15%+ of school days
Pattern Changes New Monday/Friday absences Week+ consecutive absences
Tardiness Late 2+ times per week Late daily; missing first period

Academic Indicators

Indicator Yellow Flag Red Flag
Grade Decline Drop of one letter grade Failing 2+ core subjects
Homework Completion Missing 30%+ assignments Missing 50%+ assignments
Course Failures Failed 1 course last year Failed 2+ courses; off-track for graduation

Behavioral Indicators

Indicator Yellow Flag Red Flag
Discipline Incidents 2-3 referrals per semester 5+ referrals; suspension history
Engagement Withdrawn from activities Social isolation; disengagement
Behavior Change New concerning behaviors Signs of trauma, substance use

Family Stability Indicators

Indicator Yellow Flag Red Flag
Housing Recent move; doubled-up Eviction notice; homeless
Parent Employment Job change; reduced hours Job loss; multiple jobs
Benefits Changes Benefits cliff approaching Lost childcare, healthcare, or food benefits

Staffing Model

Family Success Coach

Ratio: 1 coach per 30-40 at-risk families

Commitment: Same families for 4+ years (through key transitions)

Key Responsibilities:

  • Conduct comprehensive family assessments
  • Create family stability plans with benefits cliff maps
  • Regular home visits (monthly minimum)
  • Weekly school check-ins with teachers
  • Connect families to housing, food, healthcare, employment resources
  • Navigate benefits transitions with parents
  • Advocate for students in school meetings
  • Track outcomes in data dashboard

Required Skills:

  • Experience working with low-income families
  • Understanding of poverty dynamics and benefits systems
  • Relationship-building and trust development
  • Cultural competency
  • Data management and documentation
  • Crisis intervention capabilities

Training Requirements:

  • Empower Navigator Certification (40 hours)
  • Benefits Cliff Tool Training (8 hours)
  • Trauma-Informed Care (8 hours)
  • Motivational Interviewing (16 hours)
  • Local Resource Network Orientation (8 hours)
Critical Success Factor

The same coach must stay with families through transitions—elementary to middle, middle to high school. Trust takes time. Handoffs destroy progress.

Program Coordinator

Ratio: 1 coordinator per 4-6 Family Success Coaches

Key Responsibilities:

  • Supervise and support Family Success Coaches
  • Manage community partner relationships
  • Oversee data dashboard and reporting
  • Coordinate with school administration
  • Lead weekly team meetings
  • Manage program budget and resources

Community Partner Network

The school becomes the hub connecting families to community resources. Establish formal agreements with partners in each category:

Essential Partners

Category Partner Types Services Provided
Housing Housing Authority, Emergency Shelters, Rapid Rehousing Housing assistance, eviction prevention, utility assistance
Food Security Food Banks, SNAP Offices, School Nutrition Emergency food, benefits enrollment, backpack programs
Healthcare FQHCs, Medicaid Office, Mental Health Providers Medical care, insurance enrollment, counseling
Childcare Head Start, Childcare Providers, Subsidy Office Before/after school care, sibling care, subsidy navigation
Employment Workforce Development, Job Centers, Employers Job training, placement, benefits cliff navigation
Transportation Transit Authority, Car Programs, Ride Services Bus passes, car repair, ride coordination

Partner Agreement Template

Each partner agreement should include:

  • Designated point of contact for referrals
  • Response time expectations (within 48 hours)
  • Information sharing protocols (with family consent)
  • Regular coordination meeting participation
  • Outcome reporting requirements

Data Dashboard Requirements

Student-Level Data (from School Systems)

  • Daily attendance records
  • Course grades and GPA
  • Discipline incidents
  • Special education status
  • Free/reduced lunch eligibility
  • McKinney-Vento (homeless) status
  • English Learner status

Family-Level Data (from Navigator Assessments)

  • Household income and employment status
  • Benefits received (SNAP, Medicaid, childcare, housing)
  • Benefits cliff proximity
  • Housing stability status
  • Transportation access
  • Childcare needs (for siblings)
  • Parent education and employment goals

Intervention Tracking

  • Home visits completed
  • Resource referrals made and completed
  • Benefits transitions navigated
  • Crisis interventions
  • Parent employment changes
